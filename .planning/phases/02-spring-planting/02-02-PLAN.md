Create a comprehensive planting day session script following the template in 02-RESEARCH.md. The file must cover:

**Section 1: W16 Big Planting Day (the main event)**

Pre-planting prep (before child joins, ~20 min):
- Materials checklist: all seeds, seedlings, tools, watering cans laid out by each bed
- Pre-dig holes for strawberry plugs and sensory plant positions in Bed A
- Set out snacks and water bottle at the garden table
- Weather check note: "Postpone to W17 if soil frozen or heavy frost forecast. All crops in this phase are frost-tolerant, so light frost AFTER planting is OK."

Session 1 -- "His Bed" (Bed A, ~30-40 min):
- What HE does: places strawberry plugs into pre-dug holes, pushes pea shoot seeds into soil with his finger, places viola seedlings at bed edges, places his painted markers
- What FATHER does: digs holes, positions lamb's ear and thyme at edges, sprinkles tiny lettuce/radish seeds if succession slot is being started, positions lemon balm at corner with containment
- Transition cue: "Your bed is planted! Let's have a snack and look at it from the path."

Break -- Snack + Admire (~10-15 min):
- Sit at garden table, eat fruit/crackers
- Look at his bed from the walking path
- Count how many plants are in his bed
- "Which plant are you most excited to watch grow?"

Session 2 -- Shared Beds (Beds B, C, ~20-30 min):
- What HE does: pushes big pea seeds into holes along the trellis in Bed C (5cm deep, 5cm apart), helps carry raspberry canes to Bed B (father plants them 8cm deep)
- What FATHER does: plants raspberry canes in Bed B, sows kale and radish in Bed C, plants borage and calendula, divides and plants chives around raspberry bed
- Transition cue: "All the backyard beds are done! Tomorrow we can check if anything has changed."

Session 3 -- Terrace Beds (Beds D, E, ~10-15 min, OPTIONAL if energy remains):
- What HE does: sprinkles lettuce seeds in Bed D (broadcast sowing is fun), places viola seedlings
- What FATHER does: sows spring onion rows, plants thyme, positions borage in Bed E
- If energy is gone: "We can do the terrace beds tomorrow. The backyard beds are the important ones."

Wrap-up (~5 min):
- Put tools away together
- Final watering round of all planted beds (he uses the small can)
- Take a photo together with the planted beds
- Total target: ~75-100 min including break

**Section 2: W17 Follow-Up Session (~30 min)**
- First succession sowing: radish in marked slots
- Check germination progress in beds from W16
- "Can you see anything poking out of the soil?"
- If bug hotel is W17: transition to that activity (see bug-hotel-guide.md)

**Section 3: W18 Follow-Up Session (~30 min)**
- Second succession sowing: lettuce re-sow in Bed D
- Thin any overcrowded radish seedlings (father demonstrates, child pulls 1-2)
- If bug hotel is W18: transition to that activity
- Set up second pollinator water dish near flower-heavy beds

**Section 4: Pre-Planting Craft Activity -- Painted Plant Markers**
- When: Any evening in W15 (the week before planting day)
- Materials: 8-12 blank wooden markers, acrylic paint, brushes, newspaper/old tablecloth
- He draws or writes plant names on markers (spelling doesn't matter -- pictures work too)
- "These go in YOUR bed on planting day"
- Drying time: overnight minimum before outdoor use

**Section 5: If It All Goes Wrong**
- Rain on W16: postpone to next weekend. All crops are frost-tolerant and timing is flexible within W16-W18.
- Meltdown during planting: stop at the current bed. Whatever is planted is enough. Come back tomorrow.
- Seeds don't germinate within 2 weeks: re-sow. This is normal. "Sometimes seeds need a second try."
- Forgot to water: one missed day won't kill anything at this stage. Resume daily watering.

  <name>Task 2: Create bug hotel guide and pollinator water stations</name>

  <action>
Create a combined guide for the bug hotel/nature corner and pollinator water stations. This is a separate weekend activity from planting day (W17 or W18).

**Section 1: Nature Corner Placement**
- Choose a shady, slightly damp spot away from the raised beds -- against the fence or near the mature trees on the east/north boundary of the property (see docs/bed-layout.md for property layout)
- The nature corner should NOT be next to the beds -- creatures need their own undisturbed space
- Mark the spot on planting day (W16) so the child knows "this is where our bug hotel will go next weekend"

**Section 2: Bug Hotel Construction (~30-45 min activity)**
Materials list (all collectable from garden and nearby):
- 2-3 logs (15-30cm diameter sections)
- Handful of stones (various sizes)
- Pinecones (10-15)
- Hollow bamboo canes or plant stems (10-20, varied diameters 5-15mm)
- Dead leaves, bark, moss
- Optional: 1-2 old wooden crates or small pallet section as frame

Step-by-step build:
1. Clear a small area (~60x60cm) at the chosen spot
2. Base layer: Stack 2-3 logs horizontally
3. First fill: Wedge stones and pinecones between the logs
4. Second layer: Bundle hollow bamboo/stems and wedge horizontally between logs (for solitary bees)
5. Fill gaps: Stuff dead leaves, bark, and moss into remaining spaces
6. Optional frame: Use wooden crate(s) to create shelves for stacking materials more vertically
7. Sprinkle dead leaves around the base for ground beetles and woodlice

Child involvement -- he does:
- Collects materials from the garden (pinecones, sticks, stones) -- 15 min treasure hunt
- Chooses where each material goes ("Where should the pinecones go?")
- Names the bug hotel ("What should we call our bug hotel?")
- After building: sets up the first pollinator water dish next to it

Father does:
- Heavy lifting (logs), sawing bamboo to length if needed
- Structural stacking to make it stable

What NOT to do:
- Do not use treated/painted wood (chemicals deter insects)
- Do not use plastic (doesn't breathe, traps moisture)
- Do not disturb after building -- creatures colonize over weeks
- Do not expect instant results -- first residents may take 2-4 weeks

**Section 3: Pollinator Water Stations (2 stations)**

Station 1 -- At the bug hotel / nature corner:
- Shallow dish (terracotta saucer, old plate, or similar, 15-25cm diameter)
- Fill with pebbles/stones until they poke above the water line
- Add clean water to just below the top of the stones
- Bees and butterflies land on stones to drink safely
- He fills this when he waters the beds ("First water the bug hotel, then the beds")

Station 2 -- Near flower-heavy beds (Bed A or between Beds A and C):
- Same setup: shallow dish + pebbles + water
- Position in a sunny, sheltered spot where edible flowers (calendula, viola, borage) will attract pollinators
- This gives the child a second observation point: "Check both water dishes for bees and butterflies"

Maintenance:
- Refill with clean water every 2-3 days (incorporate into watering routine)
- Clean dishes weekly (rinse, replace water) to prevent mosquito breeding
- Replace pebbles if algae builds up (every few weeks)

**Section 4: What to Watch For**
A simple observation checklist the child can use after the bug hotel is built:
- [ ] Woodlice (look under the logs)
- [ ] Spiders (in the gaps between stones)
- [ ] Earwigs (under bark and leaves)
- [ ] Solitary bees (in the hollow bamboo stems -- look for plugged ends)
- [ ] Beetles (ground level around the base)
- [ ] Butterflies and bees at the water dishes
- "Draw or photograph any creature you find!"
