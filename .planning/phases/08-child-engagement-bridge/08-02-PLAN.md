Structure:

**Part 1: Between-Session Treasure Hunts (W18-W21)**

Introduce the concept: after planting day (W18, May 1), the garden is growing while we wait for Session 2 (W21, May 20+). Each week has ONE exciting thing to find. Format as a treasure hunt, not a task list.

Week-by-week treasure hunts with emoji icons:
- **W18 (May 1-4, right after planting):** "Can you find where you planted the sunflower seeds?" -- revisit what he planted, water his seeds, check soil is moist. The excitement of "I did that!" is still fresh. (magnifying glass emoji)
- **W19 (May 5-11):** "Sunflower sprouts are waking up -- can you spot them pushing through the soil?" -- This is the germination watch week. Also: first pea shoot harvest for a garden snack (the W19 hero task). Check if borage seedlings have appeared in Bed C. (seedling emoji)
- **W20 (May 12-18):** "Is the first radish ready to pull? Look for fat red tops poking out of the soil!" -- This is the first harvest excitement week. Radishes sown W16 are ready by W20. Also: measure how tall the sunflower sprouts are now (ruler activity begins). Check strawberry plants for first flowers. (radish/carrot emoji)
- **W21 (May 19+):** Session 2 planting day arrives! Preview: "Today we plant the tomatoes and cucumbers!"

Each week includes:
- One main treasure hunt question (child-facing, no reading required -- father reads aloud)
- Danish translation of the question for the father to use
- What to actually look for (father's guide)
- Backup activity if the main thing has not happened yet (e.g., "If no sprouts yet, water the spot and check tomorrow")
- Time: 5-10 minutes per visit, same as the daily routine

**Part 2: Sensory Walk Activities for the Gap**

Three quick sensory activities available during W18-W21 regardless of what is growing:
- Touch lamb's ear leaves in Bed A (always available)
- Smell mint in the pot near Bed A (pinch and sniff)
- Look for creatures -- ants, beetles, worms in the soil when watering

**Part 3: Photo Log Approach (CENG-04)**

Section title: "Garden Photo Log / Have-Fotolog"

The approach:
1. **One rule:** Take one photo per garden visit. That is it.
2. **Who takes it:** Father takes the photo, OR the child chooses what to photograph and father captures it.
3. **Where it goes:** Shared album (Apple Photos or Google Photos). Create one album called "Haven 2026" or similar.
4. **No captions needed.** The date metadata on the photo is enough. If the child wants to say something about the photo, the father can add a one-word caption, but this is optional.
5. **What to photograph:** Whatever caught the child's attention that day. A sprout, a bug, a ripe strawberry, a tall sunflower, mud on his hands. The child choosing the subject IS the engagement.
6. **End-of-season bonus:** At the end of the growing season (W44), scroll through the album together. The child sees his garden's entire life story in photos.

Father's notes:
- If you forget to take a photo, do not worry. No photo debt. Just take one next time.
- Do not ask the child to pose. Photograph what he is looking at, not him looking at the camera.
- This is the only digital element in the garden engagement. Everything else is physical.

Design constraints from research:
- Treasure hunt framing, not task list
- One exciting thing per week (not five things)
- Father reads the questions aloud; child looks for answers physically
- Photo log must be zero-friction (one step: take photo, save to album)
- All bed references use A-E canonical names
- Crop timing aligned with schedule data (W19 pea shoots, W20 radish harvest, W18-W19 sunflower germination)
