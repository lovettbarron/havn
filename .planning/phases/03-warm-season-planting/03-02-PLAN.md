**Document structure:**
- Title: "Warm-Season Planting Sessions -- Phase 3"
- Overview: Two sessions separated by ~3 weeks. Session 1 is quick and fun (seeds only). Session 2 is the big transplant day with a harvest celebration.
- Shopping list cross-reference: "See docs/warm-season-shopping-list.md for what to buy and when."

**SESSION 1: Direct-Sow Day (~W18, May 1)**

Duration target: 30-45 minutes. Vibe: quick, fun, big seeds only.

Weather check: Soil temperature above 10C, no frost forecast for next 5 days. Check met.dk for Vejle forecast.

Prep checklist (father, ~15 min before):
- [ ] Check soil temperature (thermometer or hand test -- should feel warmish)
- [ ] Pre-mark sowing positions in Bed A (sunflower spots at north end), Bed A edge (nasturtium), Bed E (bean row)
- [ ] Have ruler stake ready (marked in 10cm increments with waterproof marker)
- [ ] Lay out seed packets: sunflower, nasturtium, bush bean, dill
- [ ] Set out watering can

Session 1 steps with HE does / FATHER does split:

Step 1 -- Sunflowers in Bed A north end (10 min):
- FATHER: Poke 5-6 holes 2.5cm deep, 15cm apart at north end of Bed A (around row 100-120)
- HE: Drops one sunflower seed in each hole. Covers with soil. Pats down.
- FATHER: "These will grow taller than you! We'll measure every week to see which one wins."
- TOGETHER: Push ruler stake into soil next to the sunflower cluster. He holds it straight, father pushes it firm.
- Transition cue: "Now let's plant the flowers that bring bees!"

Step 2 -- Nasturtiums at Bed A east edge (5 min):
- FATHER: Poke 2-3 holes 1-2cm deep along east edge of Bed A, around (55, 40) to (55, 60), 30cm apart
- HE: Drops nasturtium seeds (big, easy to handle). Covers with soil.
- FATHER: "These flowers are bee magnets -- let's see how many bees visit! And ladybugs love them too."
- Transition cue: "Let's go to the terrace and plant beans!"

Step 3 -- Bush beans in Bed E (10 min):
- FATHER: Pre-mark a row along the edge of Bed E, 10-15cm apart, 3-5cm deep holes
- HE: Pushes one bean seed into each hole (big seeds, satisfying push). Covers and pats.
- HE: Waters the bean row with small watering can.
- Transition cue: "One more -- the tiny dill seeds. These are so small only grown-up fingers can handle them."

Step 4 -- Dill in Bed E and/or Bed C (5 min):
- FATHER ONLY: Sprinkle dill seeds (tiny -- 0.5cm deep) in Bed E near broccoli zone and optionally in Bed C near pepper zone. Lightly cover.
- HE: Waters the dill spots.

Step 5 -- First sunflower measurement (5 min):
- Walk back to Bed A sunflower spot.
- "Nothing to measure yet! But in 2 weeks, we'll see green sprouts. Same day every week, we'll check how tall."
- Optional: if child likes systems, set up a simple wall chart next to the garden entry. Date column + height column. First entry: "May 1 -- planted!"
- FATHER: Take a photo of the bare soil with ruler stake for comparison later.

Wrap-up (5 min): Final watering of all sowed areas. Walk the garden to check Phase 2 plants. "In three weeks, we'll plant the big ones -- tomatoes, cucumbers, peppers!"

**SESSION 2: Big Transplant Day (~W21, May 20+)**

Duration target: 75-100 minutes with break. Vibe: achievement day.

Weather check: Last frost has passed. Soil above 12C. PREFER overcast day or late afternoon (after 4pm) to prevent transplant shock. If hot and sunny, water beds heavily the evening before and transplant after 4pm.

Timing note: "Session 2 happens AFTER Session 1 sunflowers/nasturtiums/beans have been growing for ~3 weeks. Check their growth first -- it sets a positive tone."

Prep checklist (father, ~20 min before):
- [ ] Water all beds thoroughly the evening before
- [ ] Lay out seedlings by their destination beds:
  - Bed C: cucumber (x2), tomato Sungold (x1), pepper (x1), basil (x2)
  - Bed D: leek seedlings (x3-4)
  - Bed E: broccoli (x1)
  - Next to Bed A: mint plant + pot + potting soil
- [ ] Have tomato stake and twine ready for Bed C
- [ ] Pre-dig transplant holes at grid map positions (saves time during session)
- [ ] Set out snacks and water at garden table
- [ ] Have watering can filled

**Phase 3 Session 2A: Growth Check + Pea Harvest Party (15-20 min)**

Step 1 -- Growth check walk (5 min):
- Walk to Bed A first. "Look! Your sunflowers are sprouting! And the nasturtium seeds too!"
- Check bean sprouts in Bed E. "Your beans are up!"
- Quick sunflower measurement if sprouts are visible.
- Transition cue: "Now -- it's time for a harvest party!"

Step 2 -- Pea Harvest Party in Bed C (10-15 min):
- Walk to Bed C together.
- "Let's pick every last pea! How many can we find?"
- HE: Picks all remaining peas from the trellis. Count them together. Eat some right there.
- Celebrate: "You grew [number] peas! That's amazing. The peas did their job -- they made the soil super rich for cucumbers."
- FATHER: Cuts pea stems at ground level. LEAVE ROOTS IN SOIL (nitrogen-fixing nodules benefit cucumbers).
- HE: Helps pull pea stems off the A-frame trellis. Bundle stems for compost.
- FATHER: Light top-up with 2-3cm compost in the trellis zone.
- Transition cue: "Now the trellis gets a new climber -- cucumbers! But first, let's set up your mint."

**Phase 3 Session 2B: His Bed Additions (10-15 min)**

Step 3 -- Mint pot setup next to Bed A:
- HE: Scoops potting soil into the 30cm pot (about 3/4 full)
- HE: Places mint plant in the center of the pot
- HE: Fills around with more soil, presses gently
- HE: Waters the mint pot
- FATHER: Position pot on the path side of Bed A, next to lamb's ear and lemon balm
- "Now you have THREE things to smell every morning -- lamb's ear to touch, lemon balm to crush, and mint to pinch!"
- HE: Pinches a mint leaf, smells it. "What does it smell like?"

**MANDATORY SNACK BREAK (10-15 min)**

At the garden table. Eat snacks. Look at the garden. No tasks. Let him reset.

"After the break, we'll plant the big ones in the shared beds. You get to place them in the holes."

**Phase 3 Session 2C: Shared Bed Transplants (30-40 min)**

Step 4 -- Bed C transplants (20 min):
- FATHER: has pre-dug holes at grid map positions (see Bed C grid map for exact cm)
- Cucumber transplants at trellis base:
  - HE: Places cucumber seedling in hole at (15, 10). FATHER: backfills, waters.
  - HE: Places second cucumber at (45, 10). FATHER: backfills, waters.
  - "These will climb the trellis like the peas did -- but they'll have cucumbers hanging down!"
- Tomato Sungold transplant:
  - HE: Places tomato seedling in hole at (30, 55). FATHER: backfills, waters.
  - FATHER: Immediately installs tomato stake next to plant. Ties loosely with twine.
  - "This tomato will grow taller than you! Every week we'll tie it up and pinch off the side shoots."
- Sweet pepper transplant:
  - HE: Places pepper seedling at (30, 85). FATHER: backfills, waters.
  - "Peppers start green and turn red when they're ready. We'll watch them change color!"
- Basil transplants:
  - HE: Places basil at (15, 60) and (45, 60). FATHER: backfills, waters.
  - HE: Pinches a basil leaf and smells. "Basil helps the tomatoes grow better AND it smells amazing."

Step 5 -- Terrace bed transplants (10-15 min):
- Walk to terrace together.
- Bed D -- Leeks:
  - FATHER: Has pre-dug 15cm deep holes at 15cm spacing (see Bed D grid map)
  - HE: Drops leek seedlings into the deep holes. "Leeks grow underground -- that's why the holes are so deep!"
  - FATHER: Gently waters into holes (no backfill -- soil washes in naturally over time).
- Bed E -- Broccoli:
  - HE: Places broccoli seedling at pre-dug hole (see Bed E grid map)
  - FATHER: backfills, waters.
  - "Broccoli grows a big green head first, then after you pick it, little ones keep coming!"

Step 6 -- Final watering round (5 min):
- HE: Waters all transplanted beds with watering can
- Give each new plant a good soak -- "They're thirsty after their big move"

**Wrap-up (5 min):**
- Walk the whole garden together. Count all the different plants.
- Take a photo with the fully planted garden.
- "Your garden is complete! Every bed has plants growing. First harvest will be [timing from grid maps]."
- Set first weekly sunflower measurement day for next [day of week]. Mark on calendar.
- Creature framing: "Keep an eye on the nasturtiums -- bees and ladybugs love those flowers. Let's see who shows up first!"

**FOLLOW-UP ACTIVITIES (weeks after Session 2):**

Weekly sunflower measurement:
- Same day each week
- He reads the ruler stake at the top of the tallest sunflower
- Optional: record on wall chart if he enjoys it
- "Which one is winning the race?"

Creature observation (passive, during daily garden checks):
- Aphids will appear on nasturtiums -- this is EXPECTED and GOOD
- "The ladybugs will come -- let's check tomorrow"
- Only intervene if aphids spread from nasturtiums to food crops (unlikely)
- Borage and dill flowers attract bees -- "Count how many bees visit today"

Tomato suckering (weekly, father-led):
- Show him the side shoots growing in leaf axils of Sungold
- Pinch them out together. "We keep the plant focused on making tomatoes, not leaves."

Bean check (daily):
- Bush beans are fast growers. He can check daily for flowers, then pods.
- "Pick beans when they're as long as your finger!"

**CONTINGENCY PLANS:**

Rain on Session 1 day: Postpone 1-3 days. Seeds don't mind waiting. Don't sow into waterlogged soil.

Rain on Session 2 day: Light rain is actually IDEAL for transplanting (less shock). Heavy rain -- postpone 1 day. Seedlings can wait in their pots 2-3 more days.

Child meltdown during Session 2: Stop wherever you are. The remaining transplants can wait 1-2 days in their pots. Resume when he's ready. The pea harvest party and mint pot (his activities) are prioritized early for this reason.

Seedling unavailable at garden center: Any cherry tomato replaces Sungold. Any mini cucumber replaces Mini Munch. Any sweet pepper works. The companion planting layout works with any variety within each crop type.

Sunflowers don't germinate: Re-sow with fresh seeds. Most likely cause: too cold (below 10C), too deep, or birds ate them. Cover with mesh if birds are an issue.
