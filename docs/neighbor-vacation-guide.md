# Help Us Keep Our Farm Alive!

**A Quick Guide for Our Wonderful Neighbor**

Thank you so much for looking after the garden while we're away. Everything here is designed to be low-maintenance, but the plants do need a daily check-in during summer. The whole routine takes about **10 minutes** -- and you're welcome to eat anything you pick!

---

## Daily Routine Checklist

Run through this list once a day, ideally in the morning or evening (not midday sun):

1. **Water Bed B -- the BIG rusty-brown bed near the stairway** (the long one, 230cm). This one has a built-in water tank in the south end. Check the indicator tube on the side -- if the water level is low, pour water into the fill pipe until water comes out the overflow. Also water the soil surface if it looks dry on top, especially the north end (raspberries) which has no tank.

2. **Water Bed A -- the SMALL rusty-brown bed near the trampoline** (120cm). This one does NOT have a water tank. Poke your finger 2cm into the soil -- if it's dry, give it a good water. In hot weather, this is usually every day.

3. **Water Bed C -- the MEDIUM bed on wheels near the back fence on the cobblestones** (150cm). This one does NOT have a water tank. Water if the top 2cm of soil feels dry. In extreme heat, you can roll it into the shade -- it's on wheels!

4. **Pick ripe cherry tomatoes (tomat)** from Bed B (the big bed near the stairway). Ripe = orange to deep red, slightly soft when gently squeezed. If it comes off easily with a gentle twist, it's ready.

5. **Pick ripe cucumbers (agurk)** from Bed B -- they climb up the A-frame trellis at the south end. Ripe = 10-12cm long, firm, dark green. Pick them before they get fat and yellow.

6. **Pick ripe peppers (peberfrugt)** from Bed B. Pick when they've turned from green to red (or when they're large and glossy green if you prefer them mild). One gentle twist to snap off.

7. **Pick ripe strawberries (jordbaer)** from Bed A (the small bed near the trampoline). Ripe = red all over, comes off with a gentle tug. Don't pull hard -- if it resists, it's not ready yet.

8. **Pick ripe raspberries (hindbaer)** from the north end of Bed B (the big bed). Ripe = deep red and they come off with a very gentle tug. Check every 1-2 days -- they ripen quickly and go soft fast.

9. **Quick scan** -- walk past all 3 beds and look for anything wilting badly. If a plant looks droopy and sad, give it extra water. That usually fixes it.

**Timing:** The whole thing should take about 10 minutes. Thank you so much!

### Bed B -- "Family Bed" (the BIG one near the stairway)

**Where it is:** The large rusty-brown metal box on the **west side** of the grass near the stairway. 230cm long, 80cm wide, 40cm tall. Has an A-frame trellis over the south end with cucumbers climbing it, a staked tomato plant, and tall raspberry canes at the north end.

[PHOTO: Take a photo of Bed B showing the full length -- trellis and warm crops at the south end, raspberries at the north end]

**What's growing (south end -- warm crops):**
- 2 cucumber plants (agurk) climbing the A-frame trellis
- 1 cherry tomato plant (tomat) -- tall, staked, with clusters of small orange/red fruit
- 1 sweet pepper plant (peberfrugt) -- shorter, bushy, with green/red peppers
- Basil (basilikum) near the tomato
- Borage (hjulkrone) -- blue star-shaped flowers, good for bees
- Dill (dild) -- feathery herb
- Bush beans (boenner) -- bushy plants with small beans hanging down

**What's growing (north end -- berries):**
- Raspberry canes (hindbaer) -- tall upright stems with berries
- Chives (purloeg) -- small clumps with purple flower balls

**Watering:** The south end has a built-in water tank (reservoir). Check the indicator tube on the side -- if the level is low, pour water into the fill pipe sticking up until water comes out the overflow. The north end (raspberries) has no tank -- check soil and water if dry. Raspberries are fairly drought-tolerant, so the north end needs less water. Every 2-3 days in normal weather, daily only in extreme heat (30C+).

**Harvesting:**
- **Cherry tomatoes:** Pick when orange to deep red. They should come off easily with a gentle twist. Check daily.
- **Cucumbers:** Pick when 10-12cm long, firm, and dark green. They hang down from the trellis -- easy to spot. Don't let them get big and yellow.
- **Peppers:** Pick when they've turned from green to red (or when large and glossy green for mild). One gentle twist to snap off.
- **Raspberries:** Pick when deep red and they come off with a very gentle tug. Check every 1-2 days.
- **Beans:** Pick when pods are 10-12cm long, firm, and snap cleanly when bent. Check every 2-3 days -- picking encourages more beans!
- **Chive flowers:** You can snip them if you like -- they're edible and taste mildly oniony.

**Leave alone:** Don't move or adjust the trellis. Don't cut the tomato plant (even the side branches). Don't pull out the blue-flowered plant (borage) -- it's supposed to be there. Don't cut or bend the raspberry canes.

**Harvesting:**

**Leave alone:** Don't pull the leeks (the thick stalks) -- they need months to grow. Don't trim the thyme or flowers. The flowers (borage, calendula) are companions -- they help the vegetables.

## Emergency & Contact

**If something looks seriously wrong:**
Text [PHONE NUMBER] with a photo. Don't worry -- most things can wait until we're back. Plants are tougher than they look.

**If we've had no rain for a week AND it's very hot (30C+):**
Give every bed a deep soak -- water slowly until the soil is thoroughly wet and water starts draining out. For Bed B, also fill the reservoir pipe in the south end completely.

**If a plant falls over:**
Just prop it back up against the trellis, stake, or bed edge. A bit of string or a clip works if you have one handy.

**If you see pests:**
Ignore them unless something is being completely devoured. A few holes in leaves are normal. If a plant is covered in bugs, give it a spray with the garden hose. That's usually enough.
