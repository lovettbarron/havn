# Mulching Guide

## Overview

Mulch all 3 beds before vacation to reduce evaporation, keep roots cool, and suppress weeds during absence. Applied on **Day -3** of the pre-departure countdown, followed immediately by deep watering to settle the material and start moisture retention.

A 5-8cm mulch layer can reduce soil moisture loss by 50-70%, extending the time between watering visits.

---

## Material Assignment

| Material | Beds | Why |
|----------|------|-----|
| **Straw** | A, B, C | Better moisture retention for active veggie crops. Easy to spread. Decomposes over the season and adds organic matter. |

All 3 beds are corten steel. Straw works well for all of them. If a tidier look is preferred for Bed C (the wheeled bed on cobblestones), bark chips are an alternative -- but straw is the default.

### Shopping List

| Material | Quantity | Coverage |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Straw bales | 2-3 bales (~100L loose each) | Beds A, B, C (158-253L needed) |

Buy straw from a garden center or farm supply. Standard compressed bales expand to roughly 100L of loose straw each. Three bales provides comfortable coverage at the thicker end of the range.

## Per-Bed Instructions

**Depth target for all beds:** 5-8cm. Use a stick marked at 5cm and 8cm to check depth as you go.

### Bed A (east side of grass, near trampoline) -- Straw

- Spread straw evenly around all plants
- Keep mulch **2-3cm away from plant stems** to prevent rot
- Keep mulch away from strawberry crowns (2-3cm gap) -- strawberry crowns rot if buried
- Work around the sunken mint pot at the bed edge

### Bed B (west side of grass, near stairway) -- Straw

- Spread straw around all plants in both zones
- Keep mulch clear of the reservoir fill tube in the south zone so it remains accessible
- Tuck straw under the trellis frame at the south end but not against climbing stems
- In the north zone (raspberries), spread straw around canes and chives

### Bed C (on cobblestones, near north fence, on wheels) -- Straw

- Spread straw around all plants
- Keep mulch away from the base of small herb stems and lettuce leaves
- Slightly thicker layer (closer to 8cm) recommended -- this bed is smaller and dries faster

## Child Activity

**Framing:** "We are tucking the plants in for their nap while we are away."

Mulch spreading is a sensory-rich activity -- different textures, earthy smells, and the satisfying visual of beds transforming from bare soil to cozy blankets. The child helps with all 3 beds:

- **Bed A:** Child grabs handfuls of straw and places them around his strawberries and flowers. Father guides depth and stem clearance.
- **Bed B:** The big bed -- child works the south zone near the trellis while father handles the raspberry zone. Good teamwork activity.
- **Bed C:** The smallest surface area -- finishes quickly, good for maintaining engagement.

Let the child set the pace. If interest fades, father finishes the remaining beds while child does something else.

---

## Post-Mulching

Immediately after mulching all beds:

1. **Deep water every bed** -- soak through the mulch layer until water pools briefly on the surface
2. For Bed B south zone: also fill the reservoir via the fill tube until water flows from the overflow
3. The deep watering settles the mulch, starts moisture retention, and saturates the soil column beneath

This post-mulch watering is the most important watering of the entire pre-departure sequence.
