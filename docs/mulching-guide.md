# Mulching Guide

## Overview

Mulch all 5 beds before vacation to reduce evaporation, keep roots cool, and suppress weeds during absence. Applied on **Day -3** of the pre-departure countdown, followed immediately by deep watering to settle the material and start moisture retention.

A 5-8cm mulch layer can reduce soil moisture loss by 50-70%, extending the time between watering visits.

---

## Material Assignment

| Material | Beds | Why |
|----------|------|-----|
| **Straw** | A, B, C (backyard) | Better moisture retention for active veggie crops. Easy to spread. Decomposes over the season and adds organic matter. |
| **Bark chips** | D, E (terrace) | Neater appearance. Lasts longer (stays year-round). Heavier -- won't blow away in terrace wind exposure. |

## Per-Bed Instructions

**Depth target for all beds:** 5-8cm. Use a stick marked at 5cm and 8cm to check depth as you go.

### Bed A (backyard, west) -- Straw

- Spread straw evenly around all plants
- Keep mulch **2-3cm away from plant stems** to prevent rot
- Work around the sunken mint pot at the bed edge

### Bed B (backyard, center) -- Straw

- Spread straw around plants and along the trellis base
- Keep mulch clear of the reservoir fill tube so it remains accessible
- Tuck straw under the trellis frame but not against climbing stems

### Bed C (backyard, east) -- Straw

- Spread straw around berry plants and herbs
- Keep mulch away from strawberry crowns (2-3cm gap) -- strawberry crowns rot if buried

### Bed D (terrace, south) -- Bark Chips

- Spread bark chips evenly around herbs and sensory plants
- Slightly thicker layer (closer to 8cm) -- terrace beds dry faster due to wind exposure and smaller soil volume
- Keep chips away from the base of small herb stems

### Bed E (terrace, north) -- Bark Chips

- Spread bark chips around lettuce and quick-win crops
- Same depth guidance as Bed D (aim for 8cm)
- Check that bark does not smother low-growing lettuce leaves

## Child Activity

**Framing:** "We are tucking the plants in for their nap while we are away."

Mulch spreading is a sensory-rich activity -- different textures (scratchy straw vs smooth bark chips), earthy smells, and the satisfying visual of beds transforming from bare soil to cozy blankets. The child helps with all 5 beds:

- **Backyard beds (A, B, C):** Child grabs handfuls of straw and places them around plants. Father guides depth and stem clearance.
- **Terrace beds (D, E):** Child scoops bark chips and spreads them. Smaller beds finish quickly -- good for maintaining engagement.

Let the child set the pace. If interest fades, father finishes the remaining beds while child does something else.

---

## Post-Mulching

Immediately after mulching all beds:

1. **Deep water every bed** -- soak through the mulch layer until water pools briefly on the surface
2. For reservoir beds: also fill the reservoir via the fill tube until water flows from the overflow
3. The deep watering settles the mulch, starts moisture retention, and saturates the soil column beneath

This post-mulch watering is the most important watering of the entire pre-departure sequence.
