# Bed D: "Salad & Herb Terrace Bed" -- Grid Map

**Physical position:** Bed 4, terrace south end
**Dimensions:** 80 x 40 cm (L x W), 30 cm tall, galvanized steel
**Orientation:** Long axis roughly N-S along railing, path side = interior (house side)
**Phase 2 role:** Baby lettuce, spring onions, herbs, and quick succession crops
**Phase 3 additions:** Leeks replacing bolted lettuce zone
**Planting day:** W16 (Phase 2, mid-April); Phase 3 additions in W21 (Session 2, May 20+)

> **Remapping note:** Phase 1 assigned Bed 4 as "Herbs + sensory plants." Phase 2 expands its role to include salad crops (lettuce, spring onion) alongside herbs. Sensory plants are primarily concentrated in Bed A (His Bed); this bed gets thyme and viola as secondary sensory/color elements.

### Detailed Layout Description

**Leek zone (replacing lettuce broadcast zone):**
- 3-4 leek seedlings in a column down the center of the former lettuce area
- Planted 15cm deep in individual holes for blanching (white stem development)
- Pencil-thick transplants at 15cm spacing: (20, 20), (20, 35), (20, 50), optionally (20, 65)
- Leeks are upright and compact -- well-suited to the shallow 30cm terrace bed
- Harvest from autumn into winter (90-120 days)
- **Planting method:** Use a dibber or thick stick to make 15cm deep holes. Drop seedling in, water gently to settle soil around roots. Do NOT backfill the hole -- it fills naturally as the leek grows.

**Spring onion row (L=55cm, full width):**
- White Lisbon spring onions from Phase 2, continuing through summer
- ~6-7 spring onions per row
- Compatible with leeks (both alliums, no conflict between them)

**Edges:**
- Creeping thyme at both east corners (35, 5) and (35, 75) -- aromatic, low-growing
- Viola at both west corners (5, 5) and (5, 75) -- immediate color from seedlings

**Succession slot (10-30cm W, 70-80cm L):**
- 20x10cm section at the north end
- Continue with lettuce or radish succession through summer
