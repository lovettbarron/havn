# Slug Defense Guide

## Overview

Slug defense relies on three layers working together:

1. **Bed height (passive):** The 40cm corten steel walls with smooth vertical surfaces are the primary defense. Slugs must climb a significant vertical barrier to reach plants. This alone stops many slugs.

2. **Ferramol slug pellets (active, organic):** Ferric phosphate pellets scattered around bed bases during vulnerable periods. Organic-approved, safe for children, pets, and wildlife. Slugs eat the pellets and stop feeding. The most reliable active method.

3. **Evening patrols (manual):** Hand-picking slugs at dusk during the first 2 weeks after planting, when seedlings are most vulnerable. The child can participate as a "slug safari" with a torch and bucket.

---

## Ferramol Application

### Materials

| Item | Specification | Quantity |
|------|--------------|----------|
| Ferramol or Eco-Style Sneglekorn (jernfosfat-baseret sneglekorn) | Ferric phosphate slug pellets. NOT metaldehyd-based. | 1 box (~50 DKK) |

### When to Apply

| Period | Frequency | Why |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| **W16-W18 (spring planting)** | Every 2 weeks | Seedlings and new transplants are most vulnerable |
| **W21-W22 (warm-season transplants)** | Immediately after planting, then every 2 weeks | Cucumber, tomato, pepper seedlings need protection |
| **Wet periods (rain for 3+ days)** | Re-apply after heavy rain | Rain dissolves pellets and brings slugs out |
| **W24 onward** | Reduce to monthly or stop | Plants are established and can tolerate minor slug damage |

### How to Apply

1. Scatter pellets **around the base of each bed** on the ground, not inside the bed. Create a perimeter ~10-15cm from the bed wall.
2. Use a **thin, even scatter** -- a few pellets per 10cm, not piles. Follow the box dosage instructions.
3. Also scatter a few pellets **inside each bed near new seedlings** for the first 2 weeks after planting.
4. **Re-apply after heavy rain** -- the pellets dissolve and need refreshing.

### Safety

- Ferric phosphate (jernfosfat) is classified as organic and is safe for children, pets, hedgehogs, and birds
- It is NOT the same as metaldehyd-based slug pellets, which are toxic to pets and wildlife
- **Check the active ingredient on the box** -- it must say "jernfosfat" or "ferric phosphate", not "metaldehyd"

## Evening Patrol ("Slug Safari")

Best done in the first 1-2 weeks after each planting session.

### What you need

- Torch / flashlight (lommelygte)
- Bucket with 2cm of salty water or soapy water
- Gloves (optional -- slugs are slimy but harmless)

### When to go

- **30-60 minutes after sunset**, especially on damp evenings
- Slugs are most active on warm, humid nights after rain

### What to do

1. Walk around both beds with the torch
2. Check the bed walls (slugs climbing up), the soil surface near the beds, and underneath leaves
3. Pick slugs off and drop them in the bucket
4. Check the ground around the beds too -- catch them before they reach the bed
5. **With the child:** Frame it as a treasure hunt. "How many can we find tonight?" Count them together. He holds the torch, you pick (or he picks with gloves if willing).

### Frequency

- **W16-W18:** Every evening for the first week after spring planting, then every 2-3 evenings
- **W21-W22:** Every evening for the first week after warm-season transplants
- **After that:** Only after heavy rain, or if you notice slug damage (irregular holes in leaves)

## Passive Defenses (Already Built In)

These are part of the garden design and require no extra action:

| Defense | How it works |
|---------|-------------|
| **40cm bed height** | Smooth vertical corten steel walls are difficult for slugs to climb |
| **Companion planting** | Thyme, chives, and other aromatic plants around bed edges deter slugs |
| **Straw mulch** | Dry straw surface is uncomfortable for slugs (applied pre-vacation) |
| **Watering in the morning** | Soil surface dries during the day, making conditions less slug-friendly by evening |

## If Slug Damage Appears

Signs: irregular holes in leaves (not clean-cut like caterpillar damage), slime trails on soil or bed walls.

1. **Check Ferramol supply** -- re-apply if it has been more than 2 weeks or since rain
2. **Do an evening patrol** for 2-3 consecutive nights
3. **Check the most vulnerable crops first:** lettuce, basil, and young seedlings are slug favorites. Strawberries at fruit stage are also targeted.
4. **Beer traps (ølsnegelfælder)** as a backup: sink a shallow dish into the soil near affected plants, fill with cheap beer. Slugs are attracted, fall in, drown. Replace every 2-3 days.
