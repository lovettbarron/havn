# Slug Defense: Copper Tape Application Guide

## Overview

Copper tape creates a mild galvanic reaction with slug slime, producing a sensation that deters slugs from crossing the barrier. With proper application using 50mm or wider tape, effectiveness is 60-85%. Combined with the 40cm raised bed height (which slugs must climb before reaching the tape), this provides strong slug protection without chemicals -- safe for the child, the soil, and the food.

Copper tape is applied to all 5 beds.

## When to Apply

Apply the copper tape on assembly day, before the beds are filled with soil. This is important for two reasons:

1. **For corten steel beds (Beds 1-3):** Apply before the corten patina develops. The new steel surface is smooth and provides a much better adhesive bond than the rough, textured rust patina that develops over weeks.
2. **For galvanized beds (Beds 4-5):** The galvanized surface is already smooth -- clean and apply at any time.

If you miss the window and patina has already formed on the corten beds, clean the application area thoroughly with rubbing alcohol and consider using the optional screw reinforcement (Step 6 below).

---

## Step-by-Step Application

### Step 1: Clean the Surface

Wipe the outside of the bed wall with rubbing alcohol at approximately 5cm below the top rim. Clean a band at least 6cm wide (slightly wider than the tape) all the way around the perimeter. Wipe dry with a clean cloth.

The surface must be free of dust, moisture, and grease for the adhesive to bond properly.

### Step 2: Start at a Corner

Peel approximately 20cm of the adhesive backing from the copper tape roll. Position the tape on the bed wall, starting at one corner, with the top edge of the tape approximately 5cm below the top rim. Press the starting section firmly against the wall.

### Step 3: Apply Continuously Around the Perimeter

Continue peeling backing and pressing the tape against the bed wall, working your way around the entire perimeter. Keep the tape level and maintain consistent tension -- not stretched tight, but not sagging.

### Step 4: Fold at Corners -- Do Not Cut

At each corner, fold the tape around the corner in a continuous strip. Do NOT cut the tape and restart on the next side. Gaps are failure points -- a single break in the copper band gives slugs a path through.

For 90-degree corners, crease the tape sharply and press it flat against the new wall section.

### Step 5: Overlap at the Join

When you reach the starting point, overlap the end of the tape over the beginning by at least 2cm. Press the overlap firmly to ensure continuous contact. Cut the tape cleanly.

### Step 6: Press and Seal

Run your fingers firmly along the entire length of the tape, pressing it against the bed wall to maximize adhesive contact. Pay special attention to corners and the overlap joint.

### Step 7 (Optional but Recommended): Screw Reinforcement

Secure the tape every 30cm with a small stainless steel screw driven through the tape into the bed wall. This provides mechanical backup against adhesive failure from Danish weather cycling -- rain, frost, and temperature swings will test the adhesive over time.

Use stainless steel screws only. Ordinary steel screws will rust and stain both the tape and the bed wall.

---

## Maintenance

- **Every 4-6 weeks during growing season:** Wipe the copper tape with a damp cloth to remove the green patina (verdigris) that naturally forms. Patina buildup reduces the galvanic reaction that deters slugs.
- **After storms:** Inspect for peeling edges or lifted sections. Press back down or add a screw if adhesive has failed locally.
- **Autumn:** Give the tape a final wipe-down before winter. The tape can remain installed year-round.
- **Replacement:** Well-maintained copper tape lasts 3-5 seasons. Replace when the tape becomes thin, brittle, or extensively patinated despite cleaning.
