# Bed B: "Raspberry Bed" -- Grid Map

**Physical position:** Bed 1, backyard west position
**Dimensions:** 120 x 60 cm (L x W), 40 cm tall, corten steel
**Orientation:** Long axis N-S, path side = east (walking path between Bed 1 and Bed 2)
**Phase 2 role:** Dedicated raspberry bed with chive perimeter -- simple, perennial, low-maintenance
**Planting day:** W16 (mid-April), planted during Session 2 (after his bed + snack break)

> **Remapping note:** Phase 1 assigned Bed 1 as "Tomato bed (Phase 3)." Phase 2 moves raspberries here because: (1) raspberries must be in a separate bed from strawberries (verticillium wilt), (2) the west position gets full afternoon sun which raspberries tolerate well, (3) the self-watering reservoir originally planned for tomatoes is not needed for raspberries -- they prefer well-drained soil. The reservoir will be installed later if tomatoes move to this bed or a different arrangement is made in Phase 3.

### Detailed Layout Description

**Interior (columns 15-45cm):**
- 3 Autumn Bliss raspberry canes planted along the center line (W=30cm), spaced 45cm apart along the long axis
- Cane #1 at L=25cm, Cane #2 at L=70cm, Cane #3 at L=110cm (45cm between #1-#2, 40cm between #2-#3)
- Plant each cane 8cm deep with roots spread outward
- Canes are sturdy and self-supporting (Autumn Bliss does not require staking)
- First fruit expected Aug-Oct of year 1

**Perimeter (chive divisions):**
- 12 chive clumps arranged around the bed edges, approximately 15-20cm apart
- 4 clumps along the south edge (L=5cm), 4 along the north edge (L=115cm)
- 2 clumps on each long side at mid-points (L=45cm, L=85cm)
- Chives repel Japanese beetles and aphids -- proven raspberry companion
- Flowers in May-Jun (purple globes) -- edible and attractive to pollinators

> **This bed is simple by design.** Raspberries are the dominant plant and need space for cane growth. Do NOT add other crops. The chive perimeter is the only companion planting.

> **Raspberries are perennial.** This bed stays planted year-round. Do not disturb canes. Prune spent canes to ground level in late autumn after fruiting ends.
