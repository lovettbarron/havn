# Vacation Countdown -- 3-Day Pre-Departure Script

**Project:** Haven -- A Learning Garden
**Target:** 3 days before vacation departure (Day -3, Day -2, Day -1)
**Total active time:** ~2-2.5 hours spread across 3 days
**Audience:** Father runs the sessions; child participates with age-appropriate tasks

Three mini-sessions over three days. Each day has a clear theme, a prep checklist, timed steps with breaks, and a "done" signal. The child has ownership tasks every day -- this is about closure and care, not chores.

**Cross-references:**
- `docs/neighbor-vacation-guide.md` -- printed guide for neighbor handoff on Day -1
- `docs/warm-season-session-scripts.md` -- established session script format
- `docs/pre-departure-checklist.md` -- one-page printable checklist (tape to fridge)

## Day -3: Mulching Day ("Tucking the Plants In")

**Duration target:** 45-60 minutes including break
**Vibe:** Sensory, nurturing -- "We're taking care of them before we go"

### Prep Checklist (Father, ~20 min before)

- [ ] Straw bale/bags positioned next to beds (A and B)
- [ ] Depth-check stick ready -- a stick or ruler marked at 5cm and 8cm with waterproof marker
- [ ] Watering can and hose ready for post-mulch deep soak
- [ ] Snack and drink set out at garden table for mid-session break
- [ ] Camera/phone charged for bed photos

### Step 1 -- Bed Photos for Neighbor Guide (5 min)

**Why now:** The beds look their best right before mulching -- full summer growth, everything visible. These photos go into the printed neighbor guide.

**HE:** Takes photos of each bed using father's phone. One photo per bed, standing back enough to see the whole bed.

**FATHER:** Guide framing if needed. "Stand here so we can see all the plants."

Beds to photograph:
- Bed A (Family Bed, farther from house) -- north zone: tomatoes, cucumbers on trellis, peppers, basil, dill, borage, bush beans; middle zone: herbs, lettuce, broccoli, leeks; south zone: raspberries, chives
- Bed B (His Bed, near house) -- strawberries, sunflowers, flowers, sensory plants, spring onions, nasturtium

**Say:** "We're making a photo album of the garden so the neighbor knows what everything looks like."

> **Transition cue:** "Now let's tuck the plants in for their nap!"

### Step 2 -- Straw Mulch on All Beds (25 min)

**Who:** Together
**What:** Spread straw around plants in Beds A and B. Target 5-8cm depth everywhere.

**FATHER:** Break open the straw bale. Pull out manageable handfuls and hand them to the child.

**HE:** Spreads straw around the base of each plant, filling the gaps between plants. Use hands -- this is a sensory activity (texture, smell, the rustling sound).

**Say:** "We're tucking the plants in for their nap while we're away. The straw keeps the soil cool and wet so they don't get thirsty."

**Work order:** Start with Bed B (his bed -- highest engagement), then Bed A (the big family bed with tomatoes, cucumbers, raspberries, herbs, and greens).

**Depth check:** After each bed, push the depth-check stick through the mulch to the soil surface. The mulch should reach between the 5cm and 8cm marks.

**FATHER:** Keep mulch 2-3cm away from plant stems to prevent rot. Guide the child: "Leave a little circle of space around the stem."

**Watch for:** Straw blowing around. If windy, water lightly over the straw to settle it.

> **Transition cue:** "Great job! Let's have a snack before we give everything a big drink."

### BREAK -- Snack (5-10 min)

At the garden table. Eat snacks. Drink water. Look at the mulched beds.

**Say:** "Look how cozy the plants look now! The straw is like a blanket."

Let him reset. Do not rush to the next step.

### Step 3 -- Deep Water ALL Beds (15 min)

Walk through the whole garden. Both beds get a deep soak.

**HE:** Does all the watering. Use the small watering can for close-up work, hose for volume.

**Water order:** Bed B, Bed A.

**FATHER:** "Water slowly so it soaks in through the mulch. You'll see the straw get wet and dark -- that means the water is getting through."

**How much:** Each bed should get water until it starts to pool slightly on the mulch surface, then soak in. About 3-4 full watering cans for the large Family Bed (A), 1-2 for His Bed (B).

### Done Signal

"Both beds are tucked in and watered! The plants have their blankets and a big drink. They're ready for their nap."

**Verify together:** Walk past each bed. Child confirms:
- [ ] Mulch visible on both beds
- [ ] Depth stick shows 5-8cm
- [ ] Soil is wet under the mulch (poke through and feel)

## Day -2: Reservoir Fill and Big Harvest Party

**Duration target:** 40-50 minutes including break
**Vibe:** Celebration, abundance -- "We're picking everything!"

### Prep Checklist (Father, ~15 min before)

- [ ] Fill reservoir completely via fill tube on Bed A (north zone -- warm crops). Fill until water comes out the overflow. This takes about 10 minutes -- do it before the child joins.
- [ ] Harvest basket or bowl ready (big enough for tomatoes, cucumbers, beans, berries)
- [ ] Camera/phone ready for harvest photos
- [ ] Snack ready at garden table for the post-harvest celebration
- [ ] Kitchen colander or bowl ready inside for washing produce

### Step 1 -- Harvest Walk (20 min, 30 min max cap)

**Who:** Child leads the harvest walk. Father carries the basket and helps spot ripe produce.

**Start with his bed first** (highest engagement):

**Bed B -- Strawberries and flowers:**
- **HE:** Picks all ripe strawberries (red all over, comes off with gentle tug)
- Count berries as they go into the basket

**Bed A -- The big harvest bed:**
- **HE:** Picks ripe cherry tomatoes (orange to deep red, gentle twist) from north zone
- **HE:** Picks ripe cucumbers from the trellis (10-12cm, firm, dark green) from north zone
- **HE:** Picks ripe peppers if any have turned color from north zone
- **HE:** Pinches basil leaves for the kitchen ("Smell this!")
- **HE:** Picks all bush beans that are finger-length or longer (snap test: bend it -- if it snaps cleanly, it's ready)
- **HE:** Picks any ripe raspberries from the south zone of Bed A
- **HE:** Snips a few herb sprigs if desired (thyme) from middle zone
- Check for any ripe broccoli side shoots in middle zone

**30 min max cap:** If the child loses interest after 20 minutes, that is fine. Father finishes any remaining harvest while the child does a different activity. Do not push through.

**Say:** "Pick everything that looks ready! The plants will make more while we're away, and the neighbor will pick those ones."

> **Transition cue:** "Let's bring everything inside and see what we got!"

### Break/Transition -- Wash and Display (10 min)

Bring the harvest basket inside together.

**HE:** Helps wash produce in the colander.

**Together:** Spread everything out on the counter or table. Arrange by type.

### Step 2 -- Count and Celebrate (5 min)

**Together:** Count the harvest.

- "How many tomatoes did we pick? _____"
- "How many cucumbers? _____"
- "How many strawberries? _____"
- "What's the biggest thing we harvested?" (hold up candidates)
- Take a photo of the full harvest spread

**Say:** "You grew all of this! From tiny seeds and little plants to all this food. That's amazing."

**Optional:** If he enjoys it, weigh the harvest on a kitchen scale. "Our garden made _____ grams of food!"

### Done Signal

"Reservoir is full, and we picked everything that's ready. The neighbor will get the next round!"

**Verify:**
- [ ] Bed A reservoir filled (check indicator tube -- should show full)
- [ ] All ripe produce harvested (quick visual scan of beds)
- [ ] Harvest counted and celebrated

**Duration actual:** _______ min (fill in on the day)

## Day -1: Neighbor Walk-Through and Goodbye

**Duration target:** 30-40 minutes (neighbor portion) + 10 minutes (goodbye)
**Vibe:** Handoff, closure, reassurance -- "Our plants are in good hands"

### Prep Checklist (Father, ~20 min before)

- [ ] Print 2 copies of `docs/neighbor-vacation-guide.md` (the "Help Us Keep Our Farm Alive!" guide)
- [ ] Insert Day -3 bed photos into printed copies if possible (print photos separately and staple/clip to guide, or print an updated version with photos embedded)
- [ ] Fill in the emergency phone number on each copy
- [ ] Confirm walk-through time with primary neighbor
- [ ] Have child's shoes and sun hat ready (he'll walk along)

### Step 1 -- Neighbor Walk-Through (15-20 min)

**Who:** Father leads. Child present (shows the neighbor "his" plants). Primary neighbor follows along.

**Route:** Follow the printed guide's bed order for consistency.

1. **Start at the water tap** -- show the neighbor where to connect the hose. "This is your water source for all the beds."

2. **Bed B** (His Bed, near house, full sun) -- "Strawberries, sunflowers, and flowers. Water if the top 2cm of soil is dry. Pick strawberries when they're red all over."
   - Child shows the neighbor his strawberry plants

3. **Bed A** (Family Bed, farther from house) -- "This is the big family bed."
   - **Show the north zone:** "Tomatoes, cucumbers on the trellis, peppers, basil, bush beans."
   - **Show the reservoir fill tube:** "Pour water into this pipe until it overflows. The tank does most of the watering for the warm crops."
   - **Show the trellis:** "Cucumbers hang down from here -- pick them when they're this long" (gesture 10-12cm)
   - **Show tomato clusters:** "These turn orange then red. Twist gently to pick."
   - **Show the middle zone:** "Herbs, lettuce, broccoli, and leeks here."
   - **Show the south zone:** "Raspberries and chives down here. Pick raspberries when they come off with a gentle tug."

**Key messages during walk-through:**
- "Everything you pick is yours -- it's our thank-you for helping!"
- "Text us with a photo if anything looks wrong. Most things can wait until we're back."
- "The reservoir in Bed A (point to fill tube) means the warm crops need less attention -- just check the indicator tube and fill when low."
- "Bed B dries out faster than Bed A -- that's the priority in hot weather."

**Hand printed guide to neighbor:** "This has everything we just talked about, plus photos. Keep it wherever is handy."

### Step 2 -- Deliver Backup Copy (5 min)

**FATHER:** Walk to the second neighbor's door (child can come along or stay).

Hand over the second printed copy: "We've asked [primary neighbor] to look after the garden, but if they're away or need backup, here's the guide. Mostly just watering both beds if it's hot."

### Step 3 -- Final Top-Up Watering (10 min)

**HE:** Does all the watering. This is the last time he waters his plants before vacation.

**Water order:** Same route as always. Both beds get a thorough soak.

**Say:** "Give them a really good drink -- this is the last water from you until we're back!"

### Step 4 -- Say Goodbye to Each Bed (5 min)

**HE:** Walks to each bed and says goodbye in his own way. Do not script his words -- let him do this naturally.

**FATHER:** Follow along quietly. This is his moment.

**Purpose:** Emotional closure. Reduces anxiety about leaving the plants. He knows the neighbor will take care of them, and he has said goodbye properly.

**Suggestions if he needs a prompt:**
- "Tell the tomatoes to keep growing while we're away"
- "Tell the sunflowers to be tall when we get back"
- "Promise the strawberries you'll check on them first thing when we come home"

**Say (after):** "The plants are tucked in, the neighbor knows what to do, and you said goodbye. Everything is ready."

### Done Signal

"The neighbor has the guide, all beds are watered, and you said goodbye. We're ready for vacation!"

**Verify:**
- [ ] Primary neighbor has printed guide and completed walk-through
- [ ] Backup neighbor has a copy of the printed guide
- [ ] Both beds watered (final top-up)
- [ ] Child has said goodbye to each bed
- [ ] Phone number filled in on both guide copies

**Duration actual:** _______ min (fill in on the day)

## General Notes -- All 3 Days (2 Beds)

### Weather Abort

If heavy rain falls on any countdown day, shift that day's activities by one day. Adjust the countdown:
- If Day -3 is rained out: do mulching on Day -2 morning, harvest in the afternoon, neighbor walk-through on Day -1 as planned
- If Day -1 is rained out: do the neighbor walk-through indoors with photos, then a quick outdoor top-up watering when the rain stops

Light rain is fine for mulching and watering (less evaporation). Only abort for heavy rain that makes outdoor work miserable.

### Meltdown Protocol

If the child becomes distressed during any session:

1. **Stop the current activity immediately.** No "just finish this one thing."
2. Go inside or to a calm spot. Offer a drink or quiet activity.
3. Father completes remaining tasks without the child.
4. Do not attempt to resume the session later the same day.
5. Mark which tasks were completed on the printable checklist.

**The child's wellbeing is more important than completing the checklist.** Father can do every task solo if needed. The child's participation is valuable but never mandatory.

### Session Arc

Each day has a clear start-to-finish arc that feels complete:
- **Day -3:** Start with photos (easy, fun) --> mulch (sensory, hands-on) --> water (routine, satisfying) --> done
- **Day -2:** Start with harvest (exciting, rewarding) --> count and celebrate (pride) --> done
- **Day -1:** Walk-through (showing off the garden) --> water (familiar routine) --> goodbye (emotional closure) --> done

### Checklist Tracking

Use the printable checklist (`docs/pre-departure-checklist.md`) taped to the fridge. Check off items as they are completed each day. If a day gets split due to weather or meltdown, mark what was done and carry over the rest.
