# Garlic Autumn Planting Plan -- October 2026

**Project:** Havn -- A Learning Garden
**Planting window:** Late September to mid-October 2026
**Harvest:** July 2027
**Location:** Vejle, Denmark (Zone 7b)

---

## Overview

**What:** Plant hardneck garlic cloves in autumn 2026 for a July 2027 harvest.

**Why:** Garlic is a 9-month crop that teaches patience and long-term reward. You plant it in October, forget about it over winter, and then watch green shoots appear through the mulch in spring like magic. The scapes (curly flower stems) in June are a bonus harvest -- cut them off and stir-fry them.

**Child angle:** "We're putting these cloves in the ground before winter. They'll sleep under the snow. Then in spring, green shoots will pop up -- like the garlic remembers we planted it!" This is a lesson in delayed gratification and seasonal cycles. The 9-month wait makes the harvest feel earned.

---

## Timing for Vejle, Denmark (Zone 7b)

| Window | Dates | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| **Ideal planting** | Late September to mid-October 2026 | 4-6 weeks before first hard frost |
| First hard frost (typical) | Early to mid-November in Vejle | Ground freezing stops root growth |
| **Too early** | August / early September | Excessive top growth before winter -- the shoots get killed by frost |
| **Too late** | After late October | Not enough time for roots to establish before the ground freezes |

**Rule of thumb:** Plant when nighttime temperatures are consistently below 10C but the ground is not yet frozen. In Vejle, this is usually the first two weeks of October.

---

## Variety Selection

**Recommended type:** Hardneck garlic (Allium sativum var. ophioscorodon)

Hardneck varieties are the right choice for Danish winters because:
- They require a cold period (vernalization) to form bulbs -- Danish winters provide this naturally
- They produce scapes in June (a bonus harvest that softneck varieties don't offer)
- They tend to have stronger, more complex flavor than softneck types

**Good varieties to look for:**
- **Music** -- large cloves, robust, excellent cold hardiness
- **German Extra Hardy** -- very reliable in cold climates, strong flavor
- **Chesnok Red** -- beautiful purple-striped skin, great roasting garlic

**Where to buy:** Purchase seed garlic from a garden center or online seed supplier. Danish sources to check at purchase time:
- Local garden centers in Vejle (Plantorama, Bauhaus garden section)
- Online: floradania.dk, grontmiljoe.dk, or specialist seed suppliers
- **Do NOT use supermarket garlic** -- it's often treated with growth inhibitors and may carry disease. Seed garlic is certified disease-free.

Buy in September so you have it ready for the October planting window.

## Planting Instructions

### Step by Step

1. **Clear the bed.** Remove any spent crops, weeds, and debris. Loosen the top 20cm of soil with a hand fork.

2. **Break the bulb into individual cloves.** Do this gently just before planting -- not days in advance. Keep the papery skin on each clove (it protects against disease). Discard any cloves that look damaged, soft, or moldy. A typical hardneck bulb produces 6-10 cloves.

3. **Dig holes or use a dibber.** Each hole should be **10-15cm deep** (deeper planting protects against frost heave in Danish winters).

4. **Place each clove pointed end UP, flat end DOWN.** This is the single most important step. If you plant it upside down, it will still grow, but the stem will curve around and the bulb will be misshapen.

5. **Space cloves 15cm apart** in rows. In Bed C (150x40cm), you can fit 2 rows of 4-5 cloves. In a section of Bed B, space rows 20cm apart.

6. **Cover with soil** and firm gently. Water in well after planting -- a thorough soak to settle the soil around the cloves.

7. **Mulch immediately.** Cover the planted area with **10-15cm of straw mulch.** This insulates the cloves through winter and suppresses weeds in spring. Straw is better than wood chips for garlic (wood chips can tie up nitrogen).

### Planting with the Child

This is a great hands-on activity:
- Let him break the bulb apart (satisfying snap)
- He pokes holes with a stick or dibber
- He places each clove pointed-end-up ("the pointy hat goes on top!")
- He covers them up and waters
- He helps spread the straw mulch ("tucking them in for winter")

Total activity time: 20-30 minutes.

---

## Winter Care

**None.** Leave the bed alone under its straw mulch from October through February. The cloves are establishing roots underground even though nothing is visible above the surface. Do not water, do not check on them, do not remove the mulch.

If the straw blows off in winter storms, replace it. That's the only maintenance.

## Spring-Summer 2027 Timeline

| When | What happens | What to do |
|------|-------------|------------|
| **March-April** | Green shoots emerge through the straw mulch | Nothing -- just watch and celebrate! Point them out to the child. "Remember what we planted? It came back!" |
| **April-May** | Shoots grow taller, leaves form | Water if spring is dry (no rain for 2+ weeks). Otherwise, rain handles it. |
| **June** | Scapes appear -- curly green flower stems that loop and curl | **Cut the scapes** when they make a full curl (before they straighten). Cut with scissors, don't pull. These are edible -- chop and stir-fry, or blend into pesto. Removing scapes redirects energy into bigger bulbs. |
| **Late June-July** | Lower 3-4 leaves turn brown while upper leaves stay green | This is the harvest signal. When half the leaves are brown, it's time. |
| **July** | Harvest day! | **Pull, don't dig.** Grab the stem near the base and pull firmly upward. If the soil is dry, loosen with a fork first. Brush off loose soil -- do NOT wash the bulbs. |
| **July-August** | Curing period | Hang bulbs in a dry, ventilated, shaded spot (garage, covered terrace, shed) for **2-3 weeks**. Stems and skins dry and papery = cured. Trim roots and cut stem to 3cm above bulb. |
| **August onward** | Storage | Store cured bulbs in a cool, dry place with airflow (mesh bag, open basket). Hardneck garlic stores for 4-6 months. |

## Why Hardneck Garlic?

For the curious:

- **Hardneck** varieties have a stiff central stem (the "neck") and produce scapes. They thrive in cold climates and need winter vernalization. Fewer but larger cloves per bulb (4-10). Stronger flavor. Shorter storage life (4-6 months).

- **Softneck** varieties are what you typically find in supermarkets. Flexible stem (can be braided), no scapes, more cloves per bulb (10-20), milder flavor, stores longer (8-12 months). Less reliable in cold Danish winters because they don't need the same cold period.

For a Danish garden at Zone 7b, hardneck is the clear choice. The scapes are a fun bonus that softneck doesn't offer -- and for a child, watching the curly scapes is half the fun.
