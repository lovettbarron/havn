# End-of-Season Countdown -- 3-Day Cleanup Script

**Project:** Haven -- A Learning Garden
**Target:** W44-W46 (late October to mid-November)
**Total active time:** ~2.5-3 hours spread across 3 days
**Audience:** Father runs the sessions; child participates with age-appropriate tasks

Three days to close the garden for winter. Each day has a clear theme, a prep checklist, timed steps with breaks, and a "done" signal. This is a celebration of what grew, not a chore list. The garden is going to sleep, and we are tucking it in.

**Cross-references:**
- `docs/garlic-autumn-plan.md` -- garlic planting guide for Day 1
- `docs/perennial-care.md` -- what to cut, what to leave, and winter protection
- `docs/soil-refresh-guide.md` -- fall compost and mulch amendment (done after Day 2 cleanup)
- `docs/planting-grid-bed-a.md` and `docs/planting-grid-bed-b.md` -- what is growing where

## Quick Reference -- Motor Skill Split (End-of-Season)

| Task Type | Who Does It | Examples |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Final harvest (picking) | **He does it** | Picking last tomatoes, cucumbers, peppers, berries, herbs |
| Garlic planting (poking holes, placing cloves, covering) | **He does it** | With father guidance, see garlic plan |
| Carrying dead plants to compost | **He does it** | Armfuls of pulled-up annuals |
| Spreading straw/mulch on beds | **He does it** | Handfuls of straw over bed surfaces |
| Plant removal (pulling annuals) | **Father does it** | Pulling tomato plants, cutting tough stems |
| Perennial pruning/trimming | **Father does it** | Cutting chives, lemon balm, trimming herbs |
| Reservoir draining | **Father does it** | Opening overflow, checking pipes |
| Tool cleaning and storage | **Father does it** | Washing, drying, oiling tools |
| Structural checks (beds, trellis) | **Father does it** | Inspecting corten steel, trellis joints |
| Saying goodbye to the beds | **He does it** | His own words, his moment |

## Day 1: Final Harvest Celebration + Garlic Planting (W40-W42)

**Duration target:** 45-60 minutes including break
**Vibe:** Celebration, abundance, looking forward -- "We're harvesting what we grew AND planting something for next year"

### Prep Checklist (Father, ~15 min before)

- [ ] Harvest basket or bowl ready
- [ ] Seed garlic bulb ready (purchased in September, see `docs/garlic-autumn-plan.md`)
- [ ] Dibber or stick for poking garlic holes
- [ ] Straw mulch for covering garlic after planting
- [ ] Snack and drink at garden table for mid-session break
- [ ] Camera/phone for harvest photos

### Step 1 -- Final Harvest Walk (20 min, 30 min max cap)

**Who:** Child leads the harvest walk. Father carries the basket and helps spot ripe produce.

**Start with Bed B** (his bed -- highest engagement):

**Bed B:**
- **HE:** Picks all remaining ripe strawberries
- **HE:** Picks any remaining flowers for the kitchen table
- Count what he picks as it goes into the basket

**Bed A -- the big harvest:**
- **HE:** Picks any remaining tomatoes, cucumbers, peppers from the north zone
- **HE:** Picks ripe raspberries from the south zone
- **HE:** Snips herb sprigs (thyme, basil, chives) -- "Smell this! Let's keep some for dinner"
- **HE:** Picks any remaining bush beans
- Check for broccoli side shoots in the middle zone

**30 min max cap:** If the child loses interest after 20 minutes, that is fine. Father finishes any remaining harvest while the child does a different activity.

**Say:** "This is the LAST harvest of the year! Let's pick everything that's left and celebrate what our garden made."

> **Transition cue:** "Now let's do something cool -- we're going to plant something that will be here waiting for us in spring!"

### BREAK -- Snack and Count the Harvest (10 min)

At the garden table. Count the harvest together. Take a photo of the spread.

**Say:** "You grew all of this. From little seeds and plants to all this food. That's amazing."

Let him reset. Do not rush to the next step.

### Step 2 -- Garlic Planting (20 min)

> **Follow the garlic planting guide:** See `docs/garlic-autumn-plan.md` for full step-by-step instructions, variety selection, bed assignment, and planting depth.

**Summary of child participation:**
- **HE:** Breaks the garlic bulb apart (satisfying snap)
- **HE:** Pokes holes with a stick or dibber (10-15cm deep, 15cm apart)
- **HE:** Places each clove pointed-end-up ("The pointy hat goes on top!")
- **HE:** Covers them with soil and pats gently
- **HE:** Spreads straw mulch over the planted area ("Tucking them in for their winter nap!")

**Say:** "We're putting these in the ground before winter. They'll sleep under the snow. Then in spring, green shoots will pop up -- like the garlic remembers we planted it!"

**Framing:** This is the bridge between seasons. The garden is not ending -- it is continuing underground. Garlic connects this season to the next one.

### Done Signal

"We harvested the last of this year's food AND planted something for next spring! The garden is still growing, even in winter."

**Verify together:**
- [ ] All ripe produce harvested from both beds
- [ ] Garlic cloves planted and covered
- [ ] Straw mulch over garlic planting area
- [ ] Harvest counted and celebrated

## Day 2: Plant Removal + Composting

**Duration target:** 40-50 minutes including break
**Vibe:** Purposeful cleanup -- "We're making space for next year"

### Prep Checklist (Father, ~15 min before)

- [ ] Garden waste bags or compost pile/bin ready (if no compost setup, use municipal garden waste bags)
- [ ] Pruning shears and hand fork ready
- [ ] Gloves for father (tomato plants can irritate skin)
- [ ] Snack and drink at garden table
- [ ] Read `docs/perennial-care.md` for what to cut vs what to leave

### Step 1 -- Plant Removal (25 min)

**FATHER:** Removes all annual crops from both beds. Pull or cut at the base. Work through the beds systematically.

**Bed A annuals to remove:**
- North zone: tomatoes, cucumbers (cut from trellis), peppers, basil, dill, borage, bush beans
- Middle zone: lettuce, broccoli, spring onions, calendula, kale
- South zone: (nothing -- raspberries and chives are perennials, see below)

**Bed B annuals to remove:**
- Sunflowers, nasturtium, viola, calendula, peas, radish, spring onions

**Perennials to handle per `docs/perennial-care.md`:**
- **Strawberries (Bed B):** Trim dead leaves only. Leave crowns and healthy low growth. Do NOT cut to ground.
- **Raspberries (Bed A south):** Remove only dead/diseased canes. Leave the rest standing until February.
- **Thyme (Bed A middle, Bed B edges):** Leave in place. No autumn pruning.
- **Chives (Bed A south):** Cut to 5cm.
- **Lemon balm (Bed B, sunken pot):** Cut to 15cm.
- **Lamb's ear (Bed B):** Remove dead/mushy leaves. Leave rosettes.

> See `docs/perennial-care.md` for complete overwintering instructions per plant.

**HE:** Carries armfuls of pulled-up dead plants from the bed to the compost pile or garden waste bags. This is his main job during plant removal.

**Say:** "These plants gave us food all summer. Now they are going back to the earth to help make new soil. Nothing is wasted."

**Father note on composting:** Only compost healthy plant material. If any plants had disease (powdery mildew, blight), put those in household waste -- not the compost pile. Diseased material can spread problems to next year's garden.

> **Transition cue:** "Great job! Let's have a snack, and then the beds will be clear and ready."

### BREAK -- Snack (5-10 min)

At the garden table. Look at the cleared beds together.

**Say:** "Look how different the beds look now! They're resting. Next spring, we'll fill them up again with new plants."

### Step 2 -- Bed Surface Prep (10 min)

**FATHER:** Rake the soil surface smooth in both beds after plant removal. Remove any large root clumps or debris. This prepares the surface for the fall compost amendment (see `docs/soil-refresh-guide.md`).

**HE:** Can help rake if interested, or just observe. This step is optional for the child.

### Done Signal

"Both beds are cleared! All the old plants are composted, and the soil is smooth and ready for its winter blanket."

**Verify:**
- [ ] All annual crops removed from Bed A and Bed B
- [ ] Perennials handled per care guide (trimmed, not removed)
- [ ] Plant material composted or bagged for municipal collection
- [ ] Bed surfaces raked smooth
- [ ] Diseased material separated from compost

**Duration actual:** _______ min (fill in on the day)

## Day 3: Bed Covering + Reservoir Draining + Goodbye

**Duration target:** 40-50 minutes including break
**Vibe:** Nurturing, closure -- "We're tucking the garden in and saying goodbye until spring"

### Prep Checklist (Father, ~20 min before)

- [ ] Compost (2-5cm worth for both beds) -- bagged havekompost from garden center or homemade
- [ ] Straw bale or bags for mulch cover (5-10cm for both beds)
- [ ] Snack and drink at garden table
- [ ] Tool cleaning supplies (brush, rag, optional linseed oil for wooden handles)

### Step 1 -- Fall Compost Amendment (15 min)

**FATHER:** Spread 2-5cm of compost evenly over the soil surface of both beds. This feeds the soil organisms over winter and improves soil structure for spring.

> See `docs/soil-refresh-guide.md` for the full fall amendment protocol, including bed-specific guidance for the reservoir zone.

**HE:** Can help spread compost by the handful if interested. The texture is earthy and crumbly -- a good sensory activity.

**Say:** "This compost is food for the tiny creatures in the soil -- worms and bugs that make the soil healthy for our plants."

> **Transition cue:** "Now let's tuck the beds in with their winter blanket!"

### Step 2 -- Straw Mulch Cover (15 min)

**Who:** Together
**What:** Spread straw over both beds on top of the compost layer. Target 5-10cm depth.

**FATHER:** Break open the straw bale. Pull out manageable handfuls and hand them to the child.

**HE:** Spreads straw over the bed surfaces, filling in around perennial plants. Use hands -- this is a sensory activity (texture, smell, rustling sound).

**Work order:** Start with Bed B (his bed), then Bed A.

**Say:** "We're tucking the plants in for their winter nap. The straw keeps them warm and cozy until spring."

**Important:** Keep straw 2-3cm away from perennial plant crowns and stems to prevent rot. Guide the child: "Leave a little circle of space around each plant."

### BREAK -- Snack (5-10 min)

At the garden table. Look at the mulched beds.

**Say:** "Look how cozy the beds look under their straw blankets!"

### Step 3 -- Reservoir Draining (10 min, FATHER ONLY)

**FATHER ONLY -- child does not participate in this step.** While the child has a snack or plays nearby:

1. **Open the overflow:** Remove the overflow stub or open the overflow fitting on Bed A north zone to allow reservoir water to drain naturally.
2. **Check for remaining water:** Pour a small amount of water into the fill tube and listen. If you hear splashing, there is still water in the reservoir. Let it drain over the next several days.
3. **Leave overflow open:** Keep the overflow open or loosely covered through winter so any rainwater that enters can drain freely. Standing water that freezes can damage PVC pipes and joints.
4. **Mark for spring:** Note that the reservoir will need a fill test before the growing season starts (see `docs/reservoir-build.md` testing section).

> See `docs/reservoir-build.md` for reservoir construction details. The end-of-season note there states: "In autumn, let the reservoir drain naturally. Do not leave standing water over winter -- freezing could damage PVC pipes."

### Step 4 -- Tool Cleaning and Structural Checks (10 min, FATHER ONLY)

**FATHER ONLY:**

**Tools:**
- Clean all garden tools (fork, trowel, rake, pruning shears) with a brush and rag
- Dry thoroughly to prevent rust
- Optional: rub wooden handles with linseed oil
- Store in a dry location (garage, shed, or covered area)

**Trellis:**
- Check the lean-to trellis on Bed A east side for loose joints or damage
- Remove any remaining cucumber vines tangled in the netting
- If using removable netting, take it down and store dry
- Leave the trellis frame in place (or store if it's lightweight/removable)

**Beds:**
- Corten steel beds require NO maintenance -- the rust patina is the finish, not damage
- Check that beds are level and stable on the grass
- Check mole mesh edges are still tucked under the beds

### Step 5 -- Say Goodbye to Each Bed (5 min)

**HE:** Walks to each bed and says goodbye in his own way. Do not script his words -- let him do this naturally.

**FATHER:** Follow along quietly. This is his moment.

**Purpose:** Emotional closure. He knows the garden is tucked in and will be here in spring. The garlic planted on Day 1 is already in the ground, growing roots underground -- something to look forward to.

**Suggestions if he needs a prompt:**
- "Tell the garlic to grow strong roots while it sleeps"
- "Tell the strawberries you'll check on them first thing in spring"
- "Promise the raspberry canes you'll be back when the green shoots come"

**Say (after):** "The garden is tucked in, the garlic is planted, and everything is ready for winter. We'll be back in spring to see what comes up!"

### Done Signal

"The beds are covered, the reservoir is draining, the tools are clean, and you said goodbye. The garden is ready for winter!"

**Verify:**
- [ ] Compost spread on both beds
- [ ] Straw mulch 5-10cm deep on both beds
- [ ] Perennial plants visible through mulch (not buried)
- [ ] Reservoir overflow opened for draining
- [ ] Tools cleaned and stored
- [ ] Trellis checked and secured/stored
- [ ] Child has said goodbye to each bed

**Duration actual:** _______ min (fill in on the day)

## General Notes -- All 3 Days

### Weather Abort

If heavy rain falls on any countdown day, shift that day's activities by one day. Adjust the countdown:
- If Day 1 is rained out: move harvest and garlic planting to the next dry day. Garlic planting is flexible within the W40-W42 window.
- If Day 2 is rained out: plant removal can happen in light rain (less dusty), but skip if the child is uncomfortable.
- If Day 3 is rained out: do the mulching on the next dry day. Reservoir draining and tool cleaning can happen in any weather (father only).

Light rain is fine for most tasks. Only abort for heavy rain that makes outdoor work miserable for the child.

### Meltdown Protocol

If the child becomes distressed during any session:

1. **Stop the current activity immediately.** No "just finish this one thing."
2. Go inside or to a calm spot. Offer a drink or quiet activity.
3. Father completes remaining tasks without the child.
4. Do not attempt to resume the session later the same day.
5. Mark which tasks were completed.

**The child's wellbeing is more important than completing the checklist.** Father can do every task solo if needed. The child's participation is valuable but never mandatory.

### Session Arc

Each day has a clear start-to-finish arc that feels complete:
- **Day 1:** Harvest party (exciting, rewarding) --> garlic planting (hands-on, forward-looking) --> done
- **Day 2:** Plant removal (purposeful) --> composting (completing the cycle) --> done
- **Day 3:** Covering beds (nurturing, sensory) --> goodbye ceremony (emotional closure) --> done

### Timing Flexibility

Days do not need to be consecutive. The overall window is W40-W46 (early October to mid-November). Day 1 (harvest + garlic) should happen in the W40-W42 window for optimal garlic planting. Days 2 and 3 can happen any time from W42-W46.
