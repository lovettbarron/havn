# Warm-Season Planting Sessions -- Phase 3

**Project:** Haven -- A Learning Garden
**Target:** Session 1 in W18 (May 1), Session 2 in W21 (May 20+)
**Total Session 1 time:** ~30-45 minutes
**Total Session 2 time:** ~70-90 minutes including break

Two sessions separated by about three weeks. Session 1 is quick and fun -- big seeds only, direct sowing, done in under an hour. Session 2 is the big transplant day with a pea harvest celebration, mint pot setup, mandatory snack break, and all the warm-crop transplants. Everything happens in one area -- both beds are right next to each other.

**Shopping list cross-reference:** See `docs/warm-season-shopping-list.md` for what to buy and when. Seeds anytime before W18; seedlings in W19-W20 (as close to transplant day as possible).

---

## Quick Reference -- Motor Skill Split (Phase 3)

| Task Type | Who Does It | Examples |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Big seeds (easy to handle) | **He does it** | Sunflower seeds, nasturtium seeds, bush bean seeds |
| Seedlings and plugs (place into holes) | **He does it** | Cucumber, tomato, pepper, basil, broccoli, leek, mint |
| Tiny seeds (hard to handle individually) | **Father does it** (he covers with soil and waters) | Dill |
| Digging holes | **Father does it** | Pre-dig before each session |
| Watering | **He does it** (small can) | After each step, daily routine |
| Staking and tying | **Father does it** | Tomato stake, ruler stake |
| Pot filling | **He does it** (scooping soil) | Mint pot setup |

## Session 1: Direct-Sow Day (~W18, May 1)

**Duration target:** 30-45 minutes
**Vibe:** Quick, fun, big seeds only

### Weather Check

- Soil temperature above 10C -- use a soil thermometer or the hand test (soil should feel warmish, not cold)
- No frost forecast for the next 5 days
- Check met.dk for the Vejle forecast the evening before

If conditions are not right, postpone 1-3 days. Seeds do not mind waiting.

### Prep Checklist (Father, ~15 min before)

- [ ] Check soil temperature (thermometer or hand test -- should feel warmish)
- [ ] Pre-mark sowing positions in Bed B: sunflower spots, nasturtium at edges
- [ ] Pre-mark sowing positions in Bed A north zone: bean row (12cm intervals)
- [ ] Have ruler stake ready -- marked in 10cm increments with waterproof marker
- [ ] Lay out seed packets by their destination: sunflower and nasturtium near Bed B, bush bean and dill near Bed A
- [ ] Set out watering can (small one for him, large one for you)
- [ ] Fill both watering cans

---

### Step 1 -- Sunflowers in Bed B (10 min)

Bed B is "His Bed." Refer to `docs/planting-grid-bed-b.md` for exact positions.

**FATHER:** Poke 5-6 holes, 2.5cm deep, 15cm apart at the designated sunflower positions in Bed B.

**HE:** Drops one sunflower seed into each hole. Covers with soil. Pats down gently.

**FATHER:** "These will grow taller than you! We'll measure every week to see which one wins the race."

**TOGETHER:** Push the ruler stake into the soil next to the sunflower cluster. He holds it straight while father pushes it firm into the ground. Make sure the 0cm mark is at soil level.

> **Transition cue:** "Now let's plant the flowers that bring bees!"

---

### Step 2 -- Nasturtiums in Bed B (5 min)

**FATHER:** Poke 2-3 holes, 1-2cm deep, along the edge of Bed B, 30cm apart.

**HE:** Drops nasturtium seeds into the holes -- big seeds, easy to handle. Covers with soil.

**FATHER:** "These flowers are bee magnets -- let's see how many bees visit when they bloom! And ladybugs love them too."

> **Transition cue:** "Now let's plant beans in the big bed!"

---

### Step 3 -- Bush Beans in Bed A North Zone (10 min)

Walk to Bed A together -- just across the grass. Refer to `docs/planting-grid-bed-a.md` for exact positions.

**FATHER:** Pre-mark a row in the Bed A north zone. Poke 4-6 holes, 3-5cm deep, 12cm apart.

**HE:** Pushes one bean seed into each hole -- big seeds, satisfying push. Covers with soil and pats down.

**HE:** Waters the bean row with the small watering can.

**FATHER:** "Beans are fast growers. In a few weeks you'll see flowers, and then pods will start hanging down. Pick them when they're as long as your finger!"

> **Transition cue:** "One more -- the tiny dill seeds. These are so small only grown-up fingers can handle them."

---

### Step 4 -- Dill in Bed A (5 min)

**FATHER ONLY:** Sprinkle dill seeds (tiny -- 0.5cm deep) in Bed A north zone near the pepper area. Lightly cover with soil.

**HE:** Waters the dill spots with the watering can.

**FATHER:** "Dill is a helper plant -- it attracts tiny flying friends that protect our other plants from bugs."

---

### Step 5 -- First Sunflower Measurement (5 min)

Walk back to Bed B together. Stand next to the ruler stake.

"Nothing to measure yet! But in two weeks, we'll see green sprouts poking out. Same day every week, we check how tall they've grown."

**Optional:** If he likes systems and charts, set up a simple wall chart next to the garden entry or on the fridge. Two columns: Date and Height. First entry: "May 1 -- planted! 0 cm."

**FATHER:** Take a photo of the bare soil with the ruler stake for comparison later.

---

### Wrap-Up (5 min)

- Final watering of all sowed areas -- he uses the small can on Bed B, father handles Bed A
- Walk the garden together to check how the Phase 2 plants are doing: "Look at the peas climbing! And the strawberry flowers!"
- "In three weeks, we'll have a harvest party for the peas AND plant the big ones -- tomatoes, cucumbers, peppers!"

---

## Session 2: Big Transplant Day (~W21, May 20+)

**Duration target:** 70-90 minutes with break
**Vibe:** Achievement day -- harvest celebration followed by planting the big warm-season crops

### Weather Check

- Last frost has passed (check met.dk 10-day forecast for Vejle)
- Soil temperature above 12C
- **PREFER overcast day or late afternoon (after 4pm)** to prevent transplant shock
- If hot and sunny: water beds heavily the evening before AND transplant after 4pm
- Light rain is actually ideal for transplanting -- less shock, natural watering

### Timing Note

Session 2 happens AFTER Session 1 sunflowers, nasturtiums, and beans have been growing for about three weeks. Check their growth first -- seeing visible results from Session 1 sets a positive tone for the bigger Session 2 work.

### Prep Checklist (Father, ~20 min before)

- [ ] Water both beds thoroughly the evening before
- [ ] Lay out seedlings by their destination:
  - **Bed A north zone:** cucumber seedlings (x2), tomato Sungold seedling (x1), pepper seedling (x1), basil seedlings (x2)
  - **Bed A middle zone:** leek seedlings (x3-4), broccoli seedling (x1)
  - **Next to Bed B:** mint plant + 30cm+ pot + potting soil bag
- [ ] Have tomato stake (1.5m+) and twine/soft ties ready for Bed A
- [ ] Pre-dig ALL transplant holes at grid map positions (saves time during the session -- see each bed's grid map for exact cm):
  - Bed A north zone: holes for cucumbers at trellis base, tomato, pepper, basil
  - Bed A middle zone: dibber holes 15cm deep for leeks, hole for broccoli
- [ ] Set out snacks and water at the garden table
- [ ] Have watering can filled (both sizes)

---

### Session 2A: Growth Check + Pea Harvest Party (15-20 min)

#### Step 1 -- Growth Check Walk (5 min)

Walk to Bed B first.

"Look! Your sunflowers are sprouting!" (If visible -- they should be 5-15cm tall after 3 weeks.) "And the nasturtium seeds too!"

Check bean sprouts in Bed A north zone. "Your beans are up! Can you see the flowers starting?"

Quick sunflower measurement if sprouts are visible:
- He reads the ruler stake at the top of the tallest sunflower
- Record on the wall chart if using one
- "Which one is winning the race?"

> **Transition cue:** "Now -- it's time for a harvest party!"

#### Step 2 -- Pea Harvest Party in Bed A North Zone (10-15 min)

Walk to Bed A together.

"Let's pick every last pea! How many can we find?"

**HE:** Picks all remaining peas from the lean-to trellis. Count them together. Eat some right there in the garden.

**Celebrate:** "You grew [number] peas! That's amazing. The peas did their job -- they made the soil super rich for cucumbers."

**FATHER:** Cuts pea stems at ground level. **LEAVE ROOTS IN SOIL** -- the nitrogen-fixing nodules on the roots benefit the cucumbers that are going in next.

**HE:** Helps pull pea stems off the lean-to trellis. Bundle stems together for compost.

**FATHER:** Light top-up with 2-3cm compost in the trellis zone where the peas were cleared.

> **Transition cue:** "Now the trellis gets a new climber -- cucumbers! But first, let's set up your mint."

---

### Session 2B: His Bed Additions (10-15 min)

#### Step 3 -- Mint Pot Setup Beside Bed B

Walk to Bed B where the mint plant, pot, and potting soil are laid out.

**HE:** Scoops potting soil into the 30cm pot (about 3/4 full). Use his hands or a small trowel.

**HE:** Places the mint plant in the center of the pot.

**HE:** Fills around the plant with more soil, presses gently.

**HE:** Waters the mint pot until water drains from the bottom.

**FATHER:** Position the pot on the path side of Bed B, next to where the lamb's ear and lemon balm are growing.

"Now you have THREE things to smell every morning -- lamb's ear to touch, lemon balm to crush, and mint to pinch!"

**HE:** Pinches a mint leaf and smells it. "What does it smell like? Does it remind you of anything?"

---

### MANDATORY SNACK BREAK (10-15 min)

At the garden table. Eat snacks. Drink water. Look at the garden. **No tasks.**

Let him reset. Do not talk about what comes next unless he asks.

"After the break, we'll plant the big ones in the family bed. You get to place them in the holes."

---

### Session 2C: Bed A Transplants (25-30 min)

Bed A is the "Family Bed." Father has pre-dug all holes at the grid map positions. Refer to `docs/planting-grid-bed-a.md` for exact positions.

#### North Zone -- Warm Crops

**Cucumber transplants at trellis base:**

**HE:** Places the first cucumber seedling in the pre-dug hole at the north end of Bed A.
**FATHER:** Backfills gently around the root ball. Waters well.

**HE:** Places the second cucumber seedling at the next position.
**FATHER:** Backfills and waters.

"These will climb the trellis just like the peas did -- but instead of peas hanging down, there will be cucumbers! You can pick them right off the vine."

**Tomato Sungold transplant:**

**HE:** Places the tomato seedling in the pre-dug hole in Bed A north zone.
**FATHER:** Backfills and waters. Immediately installs the tomato stake next to the plant. Ties the stem loosely with twine in a figure-8 pattern.

"This tomato will grow taller than you! Every week we'll tie it up higher and pinch off the side shoots so it focuses on making tomatoes."

**Sweet pepper transplant:**

**HE:** Places the pepper seedling in its position in Bed A north zone.
**FATHER:** Backfills and waters.

"Peppers start green and slowly turn red when they're ready. We'll watch them change color all summer!"

**Basil transplants:**

**HE:** Places the basil seedlings in their positions in Bed A north zone.
**FATHER:** Backfills and waters each one.

**HE:** Pinches a basil leaf and smells it.

"Basil helps the tomatoes grow better AND it smells amazing. When the tomatoes are ripe, we'll pick basil leaves to eat with them."

#### Middle Zone -- Leeks and Broccoli

> **Transition cue:** "Now let's do the last plants -- leeks and broccoli, right here in the middle of the bed."

**Leeks:**

**FATHER:** Has pre-made 15cm deep holes with a dibber at 15cm spacing in Bed A middle zone.

**HE:** Drops a leek seedling into each deep hole. Just drops it in -- do not push down.

"Leeks grow underground -- that's why the holes are so deep! The deeper the hole, the more white stem we get."

**FATHER:** Gently waters into each hole. **Do NOT backfill** -- the soil washes in naturally over time as the leek grows.

**Broccoli:**

**HE:** Places the broccoli seedling in the pre-dug hole in Bed A middle zone.
**FATHER:** Backfills and waters.

"Broccoli grows a big green head first. After you pick the main head, little ones keep coming for weeks -- like bonus broccoli!"

---

#### Step 6 -- Final Watering Round (5 min)

**HE:** Waters all transplanted areas with the watering can. Give each new plant a good soak.

"They're thirsty after their big move. Think of it like giving them a drink after a long car ride."

---

### Wrap-Up (5 min)

Walk the whole garden together. Count all the different plants across both beds.

"Your garden is complete! Every bed has plants growing."

Take a photo with the fully planted garden -- **this is the Phase 3 milestone photo**.

Set the first weekly sunflower measurement day for next [pick a consistent day of the week]. Mark it on the family calendar.

**Creature framing:** "Keep an eye on the nasturtiums over the next few weeks -- bees and ladybugs love those flowers. Let's see who shows up first!"

**First harvest timeline:**
- Radish: already harvesting (from Phase 2 succession)
- Beans: early-mid July (50-55 days from Session 1)
- Cucumbers: mid-July (60-70 days from transplant)
- Tomatoes: late July-August
- Peppers: August-September (start green, turn red)
- Broccoli main head: July-August
- Leeks: autumn-winter (90-120 days)

## Follow-Up Activities (Weeks After Session 2)

### Weekly Sunflower Measurement

- Same day each week, every week
- He reads the ruler stake at the top of the tallest sunflower in Bed B
- Record on the wall chart if he enjoys it -- date + height in cm
- "Which one is winning the race?"
- Visible growth each week keeps it exciting -- sunflowers can grow 5-10cm per week in summer
- When sunflowers bloom (July-August), shift focus from height to counting flower visitors

### Creature Observation (Passive, During Daily Garden Checks)

Woven into the daily garden routine, not a separate scheduled activity.

- **Nasturtium aphids:** Aphids will appear on the nasturtiums -- this is EXPECTED and GOOD. They are trap-cropping, drawing aphids away from the food crops.
- "The ladybugs will come to eat the aphids -- let's check tomorrow!"
- Only intervene if aphids spread from nasturtiums to food crops (unlikely with the trap crop working)
- **Borage and dill flowers:** "Count how many bees visit today" -- quick daily observation, 30 seconds
- **Bean flowers to pods:** "Can you see the tiny beans forming where the flowers were?"
- Let him discover things on his own. Ask questions rather than lecture: "What do you see on the nasturtium leaves today?"

### Tomato Suckering (Weekly, Father-Led)

- Show him the side shoots growing in the leaf axils of the Sungold tomato in Bed A
- Pinch them out together -- snap off the small shoot growing between the main stem and a leaf branch
- "We keep the plant focused on making tomatoes, not extra leaves."
- This is a quick weekly task during the garden walk (2-3 minutes)
- Tie the main stem to the stake as it grows taller -- check weekly

### Bean Check (Daily, His Task)

- Bush beans in Bed A are fast growers. He can check daily for flowers, then pods
- "Pick beans when they're as long as your finger!"
- Regular picking encourages more pod production
- This becomes his daily harvest responsibility starting in early-mid July

## Contingency Plans

### Rain on Session 1 Day

Postpone 1-3 days. Seeds do not mind waiting in their packets. Do not sow into waterlogged soil -- wait for the surface to dry enough that soil crumbles when you squeeze it.

### Rain on Session 2 Day

- **Light rain:** Actually IDEAL for transplanting. Less transplant shock, natural watering. Go ahead with the session -- wear rain jackets and make it an adventure.
- **Heavy rain:** Postpone 1 day. Seedlings can wait in their pots for 2-3 more days as long as they are watered.

### Transplant Timing -- Preventing Shock

- **Best conditions:** Overcast day OR late afternoon (after 4pm) when the sun is lower
- **If hot and sunny:** Water beds heavily the evening before AND transplant after 4pm
- **After transplanting:** Water deeply. If the next day is forecast to be hot and sunny, provide temporary shade (an upturned pot or cardboard lean-to) for the first 2-3 days
- **Signs of transplant shock:** Wilting leaves that do not recover after evening watering. Keep watering -- most seedlings recover within 3-5 days.

### Child Meltdown During Session 2

Stop wherever you are. The remaining transplants can wait 1-2 days in their pots (keep them watered).

The session is structured so the high-value child activities happen first:
1. Pea Harvest Party (celebration, sensory, eating)
2. Mint pot (his setup, his sensory route addition)
3. Snack break (reset point)
4. Bed A transplants (helper role -- lower stakes)

If he stops after the Pea Harvest Party, that is a complete success. The transplants can be done by father alone or resumed when the child is ready.

**Say:** "We had a great harvest party and you set up your mint! The rest can wait until tomorrow."

### Sunflowers Do Not Germinate (Check at 2 Weeks)

- Most likely cause: too cold (soil below 10C), sown too deep, or birds ate the seeds
- **Fix:** Re-sow with fresh seeds at the same positions
- **If birds are the problem:** Cover the sowing area with mesh or fleece until sprouts are visible, then remove
- Sunflowers germinate quickly in warm soil (7-10 days) -- a re-sow in mid-May will still produce tall plants by August

### Beans Do Not Germinate

- Check soil temperature -- beans need 12C+
- Most common cause: seeds rotted in cold, wet soil
- Re-sow with fresh seeds. If soil is still cold, wait another week.
