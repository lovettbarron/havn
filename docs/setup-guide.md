# Master Setup Guide: Build Day Sequence

**Project:** Havn -- A Learning Garden
**Date:** 2026-03-26
**Review status:** Approved (2026-03-26) -- All 7 Phase 1 documents reviewed against site photos. Layout, placement, and construction plans confirmed ready for material ordering.

This guide sequences the complete garden infrastructure build from the day materials arrive through the first watering. Follow the steps in order -- each step builds on the previous one, and some things (like reservoirs and copper tape) must happen before others (like adding soil).

**Estimated total time:** 6-8 hours across 1-2 weekends.

**Before you start:** All materials should be ordered using [shopping-list.md](shopping-list.md) and delivered before starting. Bed positions should be marked out using [bed-layout.md](bed-layout.md) on the day before build day.

---

## Before You Start Checklist

- [ ] All materials from [shopping-list.md](shopping-list.md) have arrived
- [ ] Bed positions marked on the ground per [bed-layout.md](bed-layout.md)
- [ ] Garden waste collected (branches, leaves, clippings) for middle layer
- [ ] Water tap confirmed working and hose attached
- [ ] Drill with 10-12mm bit available (for reservoir PVC)
- [ ] Clear weather forecast for build day(s)

## Day 1: Bed Assembly + Reservoirs + Slug Defense (~4 hours)

### Step 1: Assemble Backyard Beds (Beds 1-3) (~45 min)

- **Time:** ~15 min per bed
- **People:** 1-2 adults
- **Child can:** Hold parts during assembly, hand over screws/bolts

Assemble the three byJEMA CUBY beds per manufacturer instructions at the positions marked from [bed-layout.md](bed-layout.md). Place Bed 1 (tomato) in the west position, Bed 2 (cucumber) in the center, and Bed 3 (berry) in the east position.

**Verify:** All 3 beds are level, positioned per layout, all sides accessible with 70cm paths between them.

### Step 2: Install Reservoirs in Beds 1 and 2 (~60 min)

- **Time:** ~30 min per bed
- **People:** 1 adult (child should step back -- sharp tools and heavy liner)

Follow [reservoir-build.md](reservoir-build.md) completely for each bed. This includes lining the bed, perforating and laying PVC pipes, installing the fill tube and overflow fitting, and filling the reservoir zone with gravel and geotextile.

**CRITICAL:** Test each reservoir with water before proceeding. Fill via the fill tube until overflow triggers. Fix any issues now -- they are impossible to fix after soil is added.

**Verify:** Water fills via tube, exits via overflow, no leaks visible. See the 4-step testing procedure in [reservoir-build.md](reservoir-build.md).

### Step 3: Apply Copper Tape to All Backyard Beds (~30 min)

- **Time:** ~10 min per bed
- **People:** 1 adult
- **Child can:** Hold the tape roll, press tape firmly against the bed

Follow [slug-defense.md](slug-defense.md) for the application method. Clean the steel surface with rubbing alcohol, apply the tape in a continuous band ~5cm below the top rim, fold at corners (do not cut), and overlap the ends by at least 2cm.

**Important:** Apply IMMEDIATELY on new steel. For the corten beds (Beds 1-3), the adhesive bonds much better before the rust patina develops. Do not wait.

**Verify:** Continuous band around each bed, no gaps at corners, overlapped at joints.

### Step 4: Assemble Terrace Beds (Beds 4-5) (~30 min)

- **Time:** ~15 min per bed
- **People:** 1-2 adults (carrying beds upstairs)
- **Child can:** Supervise placement from safe distance

Assemble and position the two galvanized beds per the terrace section of [bed-layout.md](bed-layout.md). Place them along the outer railing, directly above the carport walls and beams for structural support.

**Verify:** Beds along railing, over structural supports, level. See terrace weight assessment in [bed-layout.md](bed-layout.md).

### Step 5: Apply Copper Tape to Terrace Beds (~20 min)

- **Time:** ~10 min per bed
- **People:** 1 adult
- **Child can:** Same as Step 3

Follow [slug-defense.md](slug-defense.md). Same method as the backyard beds.

**Verify:** Continuous band around each bed, no gaps at corners, overlapped at joints.

## Day 2: Soil Fill + Trellis (~3-4 hours)

### Step 6: Fill Beds 1 and 2 (Reservoir Beds) (~45 min)

- **Time:** ~20 min per bed
- **People:** 1-2 adults
- **Child can:** Spread the top hojbedsmuld layer (fun, dirty, and manageable)

The reservoir is already installed from Day 1. Add the garden waste layer on top of the geotextile, then hojbedsmuld. See [soil-layers.md](soil-layers.md), noting the reservoir modification for these beds -- the drainage gravel layer is replaced by the reservoir system.

Layer sequence above the reservoir:
1. Garden waste (branches, leaves, clippings): 10-15cm
2. Hojbedsmuld (raised bed soil mix): 15-20cm

**Verify:** Layers at correct depths, fill tube accessible above soil line.

### Step 7: Fill Bed 3 (Standard Bed) (~20 min)

- **Time:** ~20 min
- **People:** 1-2 adults
- **Child can:** Help spread all three layers

Follow [soil-layers.md](soil-layers.md) for the standard 40cm bed:
1. Drainage gravel (16-32mm): 8-10cm
2. Garden waste (branches, leaves, clippings): 10-15cm
3. Hojbedsmuld (raised bed soil mix): 15-20cm

**Verify:** Drainage layer 8-10cm, waste layer 10-15cm, soil layer 15-20cm.

### Step 8: Fill Beds 4 and 5 (Terrace Beds) (~30 min)

- **Time:** ~15 min per bed
- **People:** 1-2 adults
- **Child can:** Help carry small buckets of soil

Follow [soil-layers.md](soil-layers.md) for the 30cm terrace bed variant with lightweight materials:
1. LECA or perlite (lightweight drainage): 5cm
2. Coconut coir or light compost: 8-10cm
3. Hojbedsmuld: 15cm

Transport soil materials upstairs in buckets -- do not try to carry full bags up the stairs.

**Verify:** Total fill height ~25-28cm (allowing for settling).

### Step 9: Build and Install A-Frame Trellis on Bed 2 (~60 min)

- **Time:** ~60 min
- **People:** 1-2 adults
- **Child can:** Hold mesh panels during attachment, test stability after

Follow [trellis-build.md](trellis-build.md) completely. Build the two mesh panels, connect them at the ridge with hinges, spread the legs to 70cm base width, and position the A-frame over Bed 2 (the cucumber bed) with legs on either side of the bed.

Anchor the legs to prevent wind toppling -- use ground stakes, L-brackets, or weighted bases.

**Verify:** Stable under firm push from each side, mesh securely attached, peak at ~120cm.

### Step 10: First Watering (~15 min)

- **Time:** ~15 min
- **People:** 1 adult or child with hose
- **Child can:** Do most of this step independently with a hose or watering can

Water all beds thoroughly from the top:
1. Water each bed until water drains from the bottom
2. Fill both reservoirs (Beds 1 and 2) via the fill tubes until overflow triggers
3. Let water soak in for 5-10 minutes, then water again

Beds will settle 5-10cm over the next week. Plan to top up with hojbedsmuld before planting (Phase 2).
