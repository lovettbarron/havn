# Bed Layout and Placement Guide

**Project:** Havn -- A Learning Garden
**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Location:** Vejle, Denmark

This document shows exactly where each of the 3 raised beds goes -- all in the backyard area near the stairway -- with dimensions, spacing, and placement rationale.

---

## Property Overview

**Orientation:** The house faces west toward the street. The backyard extends east and northeast from the house. A glass stairway on the west side of the house leads down to the backyard.

**Key features:**
- ~600 m2 total plot, 
- Backyard has two distinct ground surfaces: a cobblestone area near the north/back fence and a grass area closer to the house
- Covered terrace near the house with an outdoor water tap
- In-ground trampoline to the east/northeast of the grass area
- Mature trees cluster along the eastern and northern property boundaries, creating afternoon shade on the east side
- Roof terrace (~70 m2 above the carport) is available for seating and furniture. No raised beds are placed on the terrace.

**Sun exposure:**
- The grass area near the stairway receives good sun from the south and west -- strong growing position
- The cobblestone area near the north fence gets morning sun from the east and midday sun from the south, but may receive some shade from the fence and hedge
- East side of the backyard gets progressively more shade from mature trees, especially after midday

## Backyard Layout (3 Beds)

### Placement Rationale

The 3 beds are positioned across two zones in the backyard:

**Grass area (Beds A and B):** Positioned on the lawn between the glass stairway (west) and the in-ground trampoline (east). Chosen for:
1. **Good sun exposure:** Open lawn area with sun from south and west
2. **Water access:** The outdoor water tap is on the covered terrace, within easy hose/watering can reach
3. **Visibility:** Visible from the stairway and covered terrace, encouraging the child to check on his garden
4. **Flat ground:** The lawn is level, providing stable placement
5. **Earthworm access:** Bottomless beds on grass allow beneficial soil organisms to colonize from below

Bed B (230 x 80 cm) sits on the **west side** of the grass area, nearest the stairway. Bed A (120 x 60 cm) sits on the **east side**, nearer the trampoline. The 55 cm path between them allows the child to walk between beds and access both sides.

**Cobblestone area (Bed C):** Positioned on the cobblestones near the north/back fence. Chosen for:
1. **Hard surface for wheels:** The plantekasse on wheels rolls easily on cobblestones
2. **Separate herb station:** Keeps herbs and salad accessible without crowding the main growing beds
3. **Morning sun:** Good eastern and midday exposure for herbs and lettuce
4. **Easy repositioning:** Wheels allow moving the bed to follow seasonal sun or shelter from wind

### Bed Orientation

Beds A and B are oriented with their **long axis running north-south**. This ensures both the east and west faces receive equal sunlight throughout the day. Bed C has its **long axis running east-west**, spanning along the cobblestone width near the north fence.

## Bed Assignment Table

| Bed | Location | Dimensions (cm) | Material | Ground | Purpose | Special Features |
|-----|----------|-----------------|----------|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Bed A | Grass area, east side | 120 x 60 x 40 | Corten steel (CUBY) | Bottomless on grass | "His Bed" -- strawberry + sensory plants + flowers + sunflowers + nasturtium + radish succession | No reservoir, no trellis |
| Bed B | Grass area, west side | 230 x 80 x 40 | Corten steel | Bottomless on grass | "Family Bed" -- south zone: warm crops (cucumber, tomato, pepper, basil, dill, borage, bush beans) + north zone: raspberry + chives | Self-watering reservoir (south/warm crop zone), A-frame trellis (south end), mint pot outside east side |
| Bed C | Cobblestones, near north fence | 150 x 40 x 40 | Corten steel plantekasse | Bottomed, on wheels | "Kitchen Cart" -- herbs, salad, leeks, spring onions, broccoli, lettuce/radish succession | Wheels for repositioning |

### Assignment Rationale

- **Bed A -- "His Bed" (Child's Personal Bed):** The CUBY bed at 120 x 60 cm, positioned on the east side of the grass area nearer the trampoline. A manageable size for independent care by a 7-year-old. Strawberries provide daily picking rewards, sensory plants (lamb's ear, lemon balm) offer tactile and aromatic engagement, and flowers (sunflowers, nasturtium) deliver visible results. The east position gets good sun and sits in a high-traffic area near the trampoline -- the child sees it constantly as part of his daily play circuit.

- **Bed B -- "Family Bed" (Shared Production Bed):** The largest bed at 230 x 80 cm, positioned on the west side of the grass area nearest the stairway. The south zone holds warm-season crops (cucumber, tomato, pepper, basil, dill, borage, bush beans) with a self-watering reservoir to handle their high water demand. The A-frame trellis at the south end supports climbing cucumbers. The north zone is a dedicated perennial section for raspberries and chives -- low-maintenance and long-lived. The mint pot sits outside the east side of the bed, contained to prevent spread. Near the stairway for easy adult access and monitoring.

- **Bed C -- "Kitchen Cart" (Kitchen Convenience):** The wheeled plantekasse at 150 x 40 cm, positioned on the cobblestones near the north fence. Herbs, salad greens, leeks, spring onions, and broccoli -- crops the family can snip daily for meals. On wheels so it can roll closer to the kitchen door for herb/salad picking while cooking. The cobblestone surface provides a stable, level rolling surface. Near the back fence, out of the main activity zone.

**Infrastructure notes:**
- Beds A and B are bottomless on grass, allowing earthworms and beneficial soil organisms to colonize from below.
- Bed C has a bottom (integral to the wheeled plantekasse design) and sits on cobblestones.
- The self-watering reservoir is in Bed B only (south/warm crop zone).
- The A-frame trellis sits over the south end of Bed B.
- Copper tape slug defense is applied to all 3 beds.

## Animal Hotel Placement

The animal/insect hotel is planned for the **shaded area behind the swing set** along the far-right (northeast) fence boundary. This location is suitable because:

1. **Partial shade is preferred:** Insect hotels do best in sheltered, semi-shaded spots. The entrance should face south or southeast to catch morning warmth, but the structure itself benefits from not being in full sun.
2. **Away from main activity zone:** Keeps the hotel undisturbed while remaining accessible for the child to observe visitors.
3. **Natural setting:** The shaded, more wild-feeling corner suits a wildlife habitat better than a manicured lawn area.
