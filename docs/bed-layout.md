# Bed Layout and Placement Guide

**Project:** Havn -- A Learning Garden
**Date:** 2026-03-26
**Location:** Vejle, Denmark

This document shows exactly where each of the 5 raised beds goes -- 3 in the backyard and 2 on the roof terrace -- with dimensions, spacing, and structural rationale.

---

## Property Overview

**Orientation:** The house faces west toward the street. The backyard extends east and northeast from the house.

**Key features:**
- ~600 m2 total plot, 
- Backyard lawn area to the east/northeast of the house -- open and sunny in the west-central portion
- Mature trees cluster along the eastern and northern property boundaries, creating afternoon shade on the east side
- Outdoor water tap located in the backyard (near the house)
- Roof terrace (~70 m2, "tagterrasse") sits above the carport on the west side, facing the street
- The carport/garage (~50 m2) is the structure beneath the terrace

**Sun exposure:**
- West-central backyard receives 8-10 hours of direct sun in summer (best growing position)
- East side of the backyard gets progressively more shade from mature trees, especially after midday
- Roof terrace is elevated above surrounding trees and gets excellent sun exposure from the south and west

## Backyard Layout (3 Beds)

### Placement Rationale

The 3 backyard beds are positioned in the **north-central lawn area near the trampoline**, chosen for:
1. **Maximum sun:** Open lawn with good sun exposure, away from the mature tree canopy along the east/north boundary
2. **Water access:** The outdoor water tap is located near the stairs (by the covered terrace), within easy hose reach of this area
3. **Visibility from house:** Visible from both the covered terrace and the upper decking, encouraging the child to check on his garden
4. **Flat ground:** The lawn area is level, providing stable placement without shimming

> **Site note (March 2026):** The intended bed area is near the existing trampoline. The trampoline may need to shift slightly or coexist alongside the bed cluster. Exact placement to be confirmed with physical measurements on build day — the cluster needs approximately 4m x 2.5m of clear space. An alternative location along the left (south-east) fence was considered but rejected due to more shade and awkward fit with the raised section.

### Bed Orientation

All 3 beds are oriented with their **long axis running north-south**. This ensures both the east and west faces of each bed receive equal sunlight throughout the day. If the long axis ran east-west, the north side would be partially shaded by the bed itself.

## Terrace Layout (2 Beds)

### Placement Rationale

The 2 terrace beds are positioned along the **outer railing/edge** of the roof terrace, chosen for:
1. **Structural support:** The railing edge sits directly above the carport walls and beams -- the strongest part of the structure
2. **Wall side unavailable:** The house-side wall has a door, a window, and an AC unit blocking continuous bed placement
3. **Sun exposure:** The terrace faces west/southwest with no tree canopy overhead -- excellent afternoon sun
4. **Previous precedent:** The previous owners had two small raised beds plus outdoor furniture on this terrace

## Terrace Weight Assessment

### Weight Calculation per Bed (80 x 40 x 30 cm)

| Component | Calculation | Weight |
|-----------|------------|--------|
| Bed volume | 0.80 x 0.40 x 0.30 m | 0.096 m3 |
| Maximum saturated soil weight | 0.096 m3 x 1,700 kg/m3 | ~163 kg |
| Realistic weight (with drainage layer and lighter fill) | 60% soil + 40% gravel/air | 100-130 kg |
| Empty bed (galvanized steel) | Estimated from byJEMA specs | ~15-20 kg |
| **Total per bed (saturated worst case)** | Soil + bed structure | **~150-183 kg** |
| **Total per bed (realistic)** | With drainage and lighter fill | **~115-150 kg** |

### Weight Summary

| Scenario | Per Bed | Both Beds | Notes |
|----------|---------|-----------|-------|
| Worst case (fully saturated after heavy rain) | ~183 kg | ~366 kg | Assumes 100% soil, fully water-logged |
| Realistic (normal watering, drainage layer) | ~130 kg | ~260 kg | Drainage layer reduces soil volume by ~30% |
| Dry conditions | ~85 kg | ~170 kg | After several dry days, much lighter |

### Structural Assessment

- **Precedent:** Previous owners had two small raised beds plus outdoor furniture (sofa set, table) on this terrace. A typical outdoor sofa set weighs 50-80 kg; a table and chairs add 30-50 kg. Combined with their beds, the terrace has handled comparable distributed loads.
- **Eurocode reference:** EN 1991-1-1 specifies a minimum design load of 250-400 kg/m2 for accessible roof terraces. Two beds of ~130 kg each, spread across their 0.032 m2 footprints (each bed: 0.80 x 0.40 = 0.32 m2), produce a load of ~406 kg/m2. This is within the upper range of the Eurocode specification but should be distributed over structural supports.
- **Recommendation:** Position beds directly above carport walls or beams (along the outer railing edge), never in the center of an unsupported span. The railing edge is the strongest position because the carport walls run beneath it.
- **If using 40cm fallback height:** Each bed adds approximately 20 kg of saturated weight (0.032 m3 extra volume). Adjusted realistic total: ~150 kg per bed, ~300 kg for both. Still within safe limits when positioned over structural supports.

## Bed Assignment Table

| Bed | Location | Dimensions (cm) | Material | Purpose | Special Features |
|-----|----------|-----------------|----------|---------|-----------------|
| Bed 1 | Backyard, west position | 120 x 60 x 40 | Corten steel | Tomato + basil + nasturtium | Self-watering reservoir |
| Bed 2 | Backyard, center position | 120 x 60 x 40 | Corten steel | Cucumber + pepper + radish + borage | Self-watering reservoir + A-frame trellis |
| Bed 3 | Backyard, east position | 120 x 60 x 40 | Corten steel | Berry (strawberry + raspberry) + thyme + chives | None |
| Bed 4 | Terrace, position 1 (south end) | 80 x 40 x 30 | Galvanized steel | Herbs + sensory plants | Lightweight soil mix |
| Bed 5 | Terrace, position 2 (north end) | 80 x 40 x 30 | Galvanized steel | Lettuce + quick wins | Lightweight soil mix |

### Assignment Rationale

- **Bed 1 (Tomato):** Westernmost backyard position gets the most afternoon sun, which tomatoes need. The self-watering reservoir ensures consistent moisture during hot spells and vacations.
- **Bed 2 (Cucumber):** Center position with the A-frame trellis. Cucumbers climb the trellis and hang down for easy child picking. The reservoir handles their high water demand. Peppers, radish, and borage are companion plants that share the bed space around the trellis base.
- **Bed 3 (Berry):** Easternmost position is slightly cooler, which berries tolerate well. Strawberries and raspberries are perennial -- this bed stays planted year-round. Thyme and chives at the edges provide sensory interest and pest deterrence.
- **Bed 4 (Herbs):** Terrace position gets excellent sun for Mediterranean herbs (basil, oregano, parsley). Sensory plants (lamb's ear, lemon balm) are placed at the edges where the child can touch and smell them during terrace time.
- **Bed 5 (Quick wins):** Lettuce, radish succession sowing, and other fast-growing crops. Positioned on the terrace for daily visibility -- the child sees progress every time he walks past.

**Note:** Bed assignments anticipate Phase 2 (spring planting) and Phase 3 (warm season planting), but they are documented here because reservoir installation (Bed 1, Bed 2) and trellis construction (Bed 2) happen during Phase 1 infrastructure work. The infrastructure must be built into the correct beds from the start.

## Animal Hotel Placement

The animal/insect hotel is planned for the **shaded area behind the swing set** along the far-right (north-east) fence boundary. This location is suitable because:

1. **Partial shade is preferred:** Insect hotels do best in sheltered, semi-shaded spots. The entrance should face south or south-east to catch morning warmth, but the structure itself benefits from not being in full sun.
2. **Away from main activity zone:** Keeps the hotel undisturbed while remaining accessible for the child to observe visitors.
3. **Natural setting:** The shaded, more wild-feeling corner suits a wildlife habitat better than a manicured lawn area.
