# Bed C: "Kitchen Cart" -- Grid Map

**Physical position:** Cobblestones near north fence, on wheels (moveable)
**Dimensions:** 150 x 40 cm (L x W), 40 cm tall, corten steel (byJEMA plantekasse on wheels)
**Orientation:** Long axis E-W along the cobblestone area (330cm wide). L=0 is west end, L=150 is east end. W=0 is south edge (approach/access side, facing grass area and other beds). W=40 is north edge (fence side).
**Access:** Primarily from south side. At 40cm width, everything is reachable from the south side without stretching.
**No reservoir, no trellis**
**On wheels** -- can be moved to chase sun, rolled closer to kitchen door, sheltered during storms
**Phase 2 role:** Lettuce, spring onions, herbs, violas, calendula -- kitchen-door salad and herb production
**Phase 3 role:** Leeks replace bolted lettuce, broccoli transplant fills east section, dill and borage added
**Planting day:** W16 (Phase 2, mid-April); Phase 3 additions W21 (May 20+)

> **This bed absorbs crops from old Beds D and E.** Herbs and salad from the former terrace Bed D, broccoli and companions from the former Bed E. Bush beans moved to Bed B to avoid the allium conflict (leeks in this bed inhibit bean nitrogen fixation).

> **Wheels are the advantage.** Roll the bed to catch morning sun, move it closer to the kitchen door for snip-and-cook convenience, or shelter it against the fence during storms. All corten steel -- matches Beds A and B.

## Detailed Layout Description

### West Section: Herbs & Salad (L=0-55cm)

**Corner plantings:**
- Creeping thyme #1 at the southwest corner (5, 5) -- aromatic, low-growing, spills over the corten edge
- Viola #1 at the northwest corner (35, 5) -- immediate color from seedlings, edible petals

**Lettuce broadcast zone (10-30cm W, 10-45cm L):**
- Cut-and-come-again baby lettuce mix, broadcast-sown at 5cm density
- Harvest outer leaves from W20 onward -- the "snip and eat" zone
- **Bolts in summer heat (June+).** When lettuce bolts, pull plants and the zone is clear for leek transplants.
- While lettuce is growing, this is the bed's most immediately rewarding section: fast germination, visible growth, and edible results within weeks.

**Spring onion row (L=50, full width):**
- White Lisbon spring onions, 5-8cm apart across the full 40cm width
- ~6-7 spring onions in the row
- 60-90 days to harvest. Pull as needed for kitchen use.
- Compatible with leeks (both alliums, no conflict)
- Acts as a natural divider between the west herb/salad zone and the center leek zone

### Center Section: Leek Zone (L=55-100cm)

**Leeks (Musselburgh):**
- 3 pencil-thick transplant seedlings in a column down the center (W=20cm)
- LEK-1 at (20, 60), LEK-2 at (20, 75), LEK-3 at (20, 90) -- 15cm spacing
- **Planting method:** Use a dibber or thick stick to make 15cm deep holes. Drop seedling in, water gently to settle soil around roots. Do NOT backfill the hole -- it fills naturally as the leek grows. The deep hole blanches the stem (white development).
- Leeks are upright and compact -- well-suited to the 40cm bed width
- Harvest from autumn into winter (90-120 days from transplant)
- Leeks tolerate light frost -- they will be among the last crops harvested in the season (W40+)

### East Section: Broccoli & Companion Zone (L=100-150cm)

**Broccoli Calabrese at (20, 120):**
- 1 transplant seedling, centered in the bed width
- The tightest fit in this bed -- broccoli leaves spread 40-50cm. At 40cm bed width, leaves will overhang the sides. This is fine on cobblestones (no neighboring plants to shade).
- **Harvest the main head first,** then side shoots continue producing for weeks of extended harvest. Each side shoot is a small broccoli floret -- perfect child-sized portions.
- The single-plant approach is deliberate: one well-tended broccoli outperforms two crowded ones.

**Companion plants around broccoli:**
- Calendula at (35, 110) -- pest deterrent (deters whiteflies, attracts beneficial insects). Positioned on the north edge, slightly west of broccoli.
- Borage at (10, 135) -- pollinator attractor, beneficial insect magnet. Blue star flowers on the south edge near the east end.
- Dill at (10, 140) -- attracts hoverflies that eat broccoli aphids and cabbage caterpillars. Near the southeast corner.

**Corner plantings:**
- Creeping thyme #2 at (5, 145) -- aromatic ground cover at the southeast corner
- Viola #2 at (35, 145) -- color at the northeast corner
