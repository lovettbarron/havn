### Detailed Layout Description

**Trellis end -- south (rows 0-20cm):**
- Peas REMOVED -- cut at ground level W21, roots left for nitrogen
- 2 cucumber seedlings transplanted at trellis base: CUC-1 at (15, 10), CUC-2 at (45, 10)
- Train cucumbers up the A-frame trellis -- they replace the peas
- Cucumbers benefit from the nitrogen left by the pea root nodules

**Near trellis (rows 20-50cm):**
- Borage STAYS at (15, 30) from Phase 2 -- already established, pollinator attractor
- Borage benefits cucumbers (pollination) and is a good companion

**Center (rows 50-70cm):**
- Tomato Sungold at (30, 55) -- the star of the warm-crop bed
- **STAKE ON TRANSPLANT DAY. Prune suckers weekly to 1-2 main stems.** Sungold is indeterminate and will grow 2-3m tall. Must be staked separately from the A-frame trellis (use a tomato stake or cage, NOT the trellis).
- Basil Genovese flanking the tomato: BAS-1 at (15, 60) and BAS-2 at (45, 60)
- Both basil plants are within 30cm of the tomato -- classic companion pairing

**South-north (rows 70-120cm):**
- Sweet pepper at (30, 85) -- compact variety, compatible with tomatoes (same family, similar needs)
- Dill at (50, 95) -- near pepper, attracts hoverflies that eat aphids. Note: keep dill away from carrots (none in this bed, rule noted for reference).

## Companion Planting Rationale

| Pairing | Relationship | Status |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Tomato + basil | Classic companion: basil improves tomato flavor, repels aphids/whiteflies | GOOD |
| Cucumber + borage | Borage attracts pollinators for cucumber flowers, may improve flavor | GOOD |
| Dill + pepper | Dill attracts hoverflies that eat aphids near pepper plants | GOOD |
| Tomato + cucumber | Workable in same bed with adequate spacing and vertical growing; both need consistent water | ACCEPTABLE |
| Pepper + tomato | Same family (solanaceae), similar water/nutrient needs; compact pepper works alongside staked tomato | GOOD |
| Basil + cucumber | Neutral to beneficial -- no conflict, basil may deter some cucumber pests | GOOD |
| No alliums in this bed | Beans are in Bed E -- if leeks were in this bed, they would inhibit bean growth in Bed E indirectly (no direct conflict, but keeping alliums consolidated in Bed D) | ENFORCED |
