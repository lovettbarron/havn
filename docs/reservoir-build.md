# Self-Watering Reservoir Build Guide

## Overview

A sub-irrigated planter (SIP) uses a PVC pipe reservoir at the bottom of the bed. Water is poured into a vertical fill tube and distributes through perforated pipes surrounded by gravel. The water then wicks upward through the soil via capillary action, keeping roots consistently moist from below.

Benefits:
- **2-4 days of water autonomy** per fill, depending on temperature and plant size
- **Reduced surface moisture** -- the soil surface stays drier, which discourages slugs and fungal diseases
- **Vacation survival** -- fill the reservoir before leaving and the beds sustain themselves for days

Install reservoirs in **Bed 1 (tomato)** and **Bed 2 (cucumber)** only. These are the two highest-water-demand crops and the ones that must survive vacation periods.

## Materials Needed (Per Bed)

| Item | Specification | Quantity |
|------|--------------|----------|
| PVC pipe | 50mm diameter | 3m |
| PVC 90-degree elbows | 50mm | 2 |
| PVC end caps | 50mm | 2 |
| Vertical fill tube | 50mm PVC, 50cm length | 1 |
| Pond liner / EPDM | 0.5mm+ thickness, sized 150cm x 90cm (bed + 30cm overlap on each side) | 1 sheet |
| Geotextile fabric | Water-permeable, soil-blocking | 1 piece, 120cm x 60cm (bed floor size) |
| Coarse gravel | 16-32mm | ~30 liters |
| Overflow fitting | Short PVC stub or bulkhead fitting | 1 |
| Drill with 10-12mm bit | For perforating pipes and overflow hole | 1 |
| PVC cement (optional) | Dry-fit works for garden use | 1 tube |

**Total materials for both beds:** Double everything above. Budget approximately 500-800 DKK for both beds combined.

## Build Sequence (Per Bed)

### Step 1: Line the Bed with Pond Liner

Lay the EPDM/pond liner inside the bed so it covers the entire floor and folds up the bed walls to at least 10cm above the bed floor. The liner creates the waterproof reservoir zone -- water must stay in the bottom of the bed, not leak out through the metal walls.

Smooth the liner flat against the floor and press it into the corners. Avoid creasing it sharply -- gentle folds are fine.

### Step 2: Perforate the PVC Pipes

Cut the PVC pipes to bed length, approximately 110cm each (leaving room at the ends). You need 2-3 pipes per bed.

Drill 10-12mm holes every 10cm along the **top half** of each pipe. These holes distribute water from inside the pipe into the surrounding gravel. Drilling only on the top half prevents gravel from blocking the holes -- water rises out of the pipe rather than gravel falling in.

### Step 3: Lay the Pipes on the Bed Floor

Place 2-3 perforated PVC pipes parallel across the bed floor on top of the liner, spaced evenly (approximately 15-20cm apart). Cap both ends with PVC end caps.

You can either:
- **Independent channels:** Leave each pipe separate (simpler)
- **Connected loop:** Join pipes at one end using 90-degree elbows to create a U-shaped circuit (better water distribution)

For most gardens, independent channels work well.

### Step 4: Install the Vertical Fill Tube

Connect a 50cm vertical PVC pipe to one end of the horizontal pipe system using a 90-degree elbow. This tube extends above the final soil line -- it is where you pour water to fill the reservoir.

Position the fill tube near a corner of the bed for easy access. The top of the tube should extend approximately 5-10cm above the final soil surface. You can mark the fill tube with tape or paint so it is easy to identify.

### Step 5: Install the Overflow

This is the most critical step. The overflow prevents the reservoir from flooding the soil zone above it.

Drill or cut a hole through the bed wall AND the pond liner at exactly 8cm above the bed floor. Insert a short PVC stub (5-10cm length) through the hole so it protrudes slightly on the outside. Seal around the stub with silicone or PVC cement to prevent leaking at the joint.

**CRITICAL:** The overflow MUST work. Test it with water before adding any soil:
1. Block the overflow temporarily (finger or tape)
2. Pour water through the fill tube
3. Watch the water level rise to 8cm
4. Release the overflow -- water should flow out freely

If the overflow is blocked or too small, the reservoir will flood the soil above and cause root rot.

### Step 6: Fill with Gravel

Pour coarse gravel (16-32mm) around and over the PVC pipes until the gravel is level with the top of the pipes, approximately 8cm deep. The gravel creates reservoir volume in the spaces between the stones.

Spread evenly and avoid shifting the pipes. The gravel layer should completely cover the pipes.

### Step 7: Lay Geotextile Fabric

Lay the geotextile fabric over the entire gravel layer, covering it completely. The fabric:
- **Prevents** soil from washing down into the gravel and clogging the reservoir
- **Allows** water to wick upward through the fabric into the soil above

Tuck the edges of the fabric against the bed walls. It does not need to be sealed -- gravity keeps soil from falling through.

### Step 8: Continue with Normal Soil Layers

The reservoir replaces the drainage layer described in [soil-layers.md](soil-layers.md). On top of the geotextile, add:
- **10-15cm garden waste** (branches, leaves, grass clippings)
- **15-20cm hojbedsmuld** (top growing layer)

The total bed depth remains 40cm: approximately 8cm reservoir + 10-15cm garden waste + 15-20cm hojbedsmuld.

## Testing the Reservoir

Perform this test BEFORE adding any soil layers above the geotextile.

1. **Fill test:** Pour water into the fill tube until water flows from the overflow hole. Note how much water this takes -- it should be approximately **15-20 liters** per bed.
2. **Hold test:** Wait 30 minutes. Check that the gravel zone is saturated (water visible between stones) and that no water is pooling on top of the geotextile.
3. **Drainage test:** If water pools on top of the geotextile, the fabric may be too dense. Replace with a more permeable grade -- you need water to wick UP through it, not pool on the surface.
4. **Overflow test:** Confirm the overflow drains freely when the reservoir is full. Water should drip or trickle from the outside of the bed at the overflow point.

Only proceed with soil filling after all four tests pass.

## Usage During Growing Season

- **Fill frequency:** Pour water into the fill tube every 2-4 days. In hot weather (25C+) or when plants are large and fruiting, fill every 1-2 days. In rainy periods, skip filling -- the overflow handles excess.
- **Dry surface is normal:** The soil surface may look and feel dry while roots are well-watered from below. This is a feature, not a problem -- dry surfaces discourage slugs and reduce fungal disease.
- **How to check water level:** Pour a small amount of water into the fill tube and listen. If you hear splashing, the reservoir is partially empty. If water immediately appears at the overflow, it is full.
- **Before vacation:** Fill the reservoir completely AND water from the top as well. This saturates the entire soil column, maximizing water reserves. A full reservoir plus saturated soil can sustain tomatoes and cucumbers for 3-5 days in moderate weather.
- **End of season:** In autumn, let the reservoir drain naturally. Do not leave standing water over winter -- freezing could damage PVC pipes. If possible, tip the bed slightly or remove the overflow stub to drain fully.
